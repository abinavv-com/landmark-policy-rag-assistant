# Holiday and Public Observance Operating Hours Policy

This document covers how store hours, delivery timelines, and customer service availability change around major public holidays and observances in the UAE, KSA, and India, across all six Landmark Group brands (Lifestyle, Max, Splash, Home Centre, Centrepoint, Babyshop).

## Store Hours Around Major Holidays

Store operating hours vary from standard hours around each market's major public holidays and religious observances, and the change depends on the specific occasion:

- **UAE and KSA (Eid al-Fitr, Eid al-Adha, UAE National Day, Saudi National Day)**: Most mall-based stores extend operating hours in the days immediately before Eid, reflecting the pre-Eid shopping surge, opening earlier and closing later than standard hours. On the Eid public holidays themselves, store hours follow the host mall's own holiday schedule, typically a modified, shorter window rather than fully closed. During Ramadan, standard store hours across the UAE and KSA extend into the late evening in line with mall-wide Ramadan hours, consistent with the elevated campaign period referenced in the Sale Event Rules document.
- **India (Diwali, Holi, and regional festivals)**: Standalone stores generally extend hours in the run-up to Diwali given the strong festive shopping pattern, while remaining open on Diwali day itself, since it is one of the highest-footfall retail days of the year rather than a closure day. Mall-based stores follow the host mall's holiday hours. Regional festivals (Pongal, Onam, Durga Puja) affect hours only in the states or cities where observed, and store-specific hours are published on the store locator closer to the date.
- **National Day observances**: UAE National Day (December 2-3) and Saudi National Day (September 23) typically see standard-to-extended mall hours rather than closures.

Customers should check the specific store's hours via the store locator on the app or website in the days immediately before a major holiday, since exact extended or modified hours are set per mall or per standalone location and are not identical across every store in a market.

## Delivery Timeline Impact Around Holidays

The standard delivery timelines described in the Shipping and Delivery FAQ assume normal business-day courier operations; timelines extend around major holidays in each market due to reduced courier and warehouse staffing:

- Orders placed 3-5 days before Eid al-Fitr, Eid al-Adha, or Diwali should expect delivery timelines to run 1-3 business days longer than the standard stated window, given peak order volume plus reduced courier capacity on the holiday days themselves.
- Public holidays are excluded from the business-day count used to calculate delivery estimates across all three markets; a holiday within an order's transit window simply does not count as a business day, without representing an actual service failure.
- Express and same-day delivery, where available, is typically suspended entirely on the public holiday itself (for example, no same-day delivery in Dubai on Eid or UAE National Day), disclosed at checkout when an order falls close enough to be affected.
- Click-and-collect orders are unaffected by courier capacity but are still subject to the collecting store's own holiday hours.

## Customer Service Availability During Holidays

Standard CX hours (8:00 AM-11:00 PM local time across all three markets, as described in the Complaint Escalation Process) are maintained on most public holidays with normal staffing, since digital and phone-based CX operates independently of physical store and mall hours. On the specific days of Eid al-Fitr and Eid al-Adha (typically the first day of each), and on Diwali day in India, CX operates on a reduced-staffing holiday schedule with longer response times than the standard Tier 1 SLA (chat response may extend beyond the normal 2-hour SLA, and email response may extend beyond the normal 24-hour SLA), while remaining available for urgent cases such as a lost or damaged delivery. This reduced-staffing schedule is communicated in advance via an in-app banner in the days leading up to the holiday.

Social media care, given its priority SLA for public visibility reasons as described in the Complaint Escalation Process, maintains closer-to-standard response times even during reduced-staffing holiday days, since social complaints are monitored by a rotating on-call team rather than the full standard shift roster.

## Corporate Orders and Planning Guidance

Corporate bulk orders and corporate holiday gifting orders, as referenced in the Festive and Gifting Services FAQ, must account for the same holiday-driven capacity constraints in addition to the standard 15-business-day advance-booking guidance already given for festive-period corporate gifting; a corporate order intended for delivery on or immediately before a major holiday should ideally be placed even earlier than the standard guidance where possible, since courier and fulfillment capacity is shared between individual consumer orders and bulk corporate orders during the same peak window.

Customers with a time-sensitive need around any of the above holiday periods should be advised by CX to place their order with additional buffer time beyond the standard delivery estimate shown at checkout, and to consider click-and-collect at a store with confirmed extended hours as a more time-certain alternative to home delivery during the highest-volume days immediately preceding Eid, Diwali, or the National Day periods.
