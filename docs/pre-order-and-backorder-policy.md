# Pre-Order and Backorder Policy

This document covers how pre-orders work for upcoming collection drops, how backorders are handled when in-stock items become temporarily unavailable after purchase, and the cancellation rights specific to both, across Landmark Group brands (Lifestyle, Max, Splash, Home Centre, Centrepoint, Babyshop) in the UAE, KSA, and India.

## How Pre-Orders Work

Pre-orders are opened for select new collection drops — seasonal launches, brand collaborations, or high-demand restocks — ahead of the item physically arriving in Landmark's warehouse. A product page open for pre-order is clearly labeled "Pre-Order" rather than "Add to Cart," and displays an estimated ship window (for example, "Ships in 2-3 weeks") rather than a fixed delivery date, since the estimate depends on the supplier's confirmed inbound shipment schedule.

Pre-order quantities are allocated on a first-confirmed, first-served basis against the supplier's expected inbound stock, meaning a pre-order placed earlier is prioritized for fulfillment ahead of one placed later in the same window, in the rare event that inbound quantity falls short of total pre-orders taken.

## Estimated Ship Dates and Delay Communication

The estimated ship window shown at the time of pre-order is based on the supplier's latest confirmed production and shipping schedule at that point, but is not a guaranteed delivery date. If the estimated ship date shifts — most commonly due to a supplier production delay or a customs clearance delay on an imported collection — Landmark notifies all affected pre-order customers by app notification, SMS, and email as soon as the delay is confirmed internally, rather than waiting until the original estimated date has already passed.

Delay notifications state the revised estimated ship window and, for delays exceeding 14 days beyond the original estimate, automatically offer the customer the option to cancel for a full refund without needing to contact CX, in addition to the option to continue waiting for the item.

## Payment Timing for Pre-Orders

Pre-orders are charged in full at the time the order is placed, not at the time of shipment, since this reserves the customer's allocation against the confirmed inbound quantity. This differs from a simple backorder (see below), where payment timing follows the standard in-stock checkout flow. Pre-order customers using Tabby or Tamara BNPL have their installment schedule begin from the pre-order placement date, not from the eventual ship date, consistent with how BNPL is scheduled on any standard order.

## Backorder Policy

A backorder differs from a pre-order: it occurs when an item that was in stock and available for standard checkout becomes temporarily unavailable after the customer's order is placed but before it ships, typically due to a warehouse stock count discrepancy or an unexpected demand spike depleting inventory faster than the system reflects. Payment for a backordered item is collected at the time of the original order, matching standard checkout behavior, since the item was believed to be in stock when payment was taken.

When an ordered item goes on backorder, the customer is notified within 24 hours of the discrepancy being identified, with a revised estimated ship date based on the next confirmed restock. If the item is part of a multi-item order, the in-stock items ship immediately (or per the customer's original delivery preference) while the backordered item ships separately once restocked, at no additional shipping cost to the customer for the split shipment.

## Cancellation Rights

**Pre-orders**: Customers may cancel a pre-order at any time before it ships, for a full refund, since no physical fulfillment has begun. This is a broader cancellation right than the standard 1-hour instant-cancellation rule that applies to in-stock orders, because a pre-order's extended lead time means the picking/packing stage does not begin until stock actually arrives.

**Backorders**: Customers may similarly cancel the backordered portion of their order at any time before it ships, for a full refund of that item, without needing to cancel or affect the rest of the order. If a backorder delay extends beyond 21 days from the original promised delivery date, Landmark proactively offers the customer a full refund option for the delayed item alongside the choice to keep waiting, communicated the same way as a pre-order delay notification.

In both cases, refunds for cancelled pre-orders or backorders follow the standard method-based refund timelines (5-7 business days for cards, 3-5 business days for wallet or Landmark Rewards balance payments), and any Landmark Rewards points held pending on the cancelled item are reversed at the time the refund is processed, since points are only finalized once an order ships and is not subsequently cancelled or returned.
