# Gift Cards and Vouchers Policy

This document covers gift card formats, denominations, expiry, redemption rules, and lost-card handling across Landmark Group brands (Lifestyle, Max, Splash, Home Centre, Centrepoint, Babyshop) in the UAE, KSA, and India. It also distinguishes gift cards from promotional vouchers, which follow different rules.

## Physical vs. E-Gift Cards

Physical gift cards are plastic cards purchased and activated in-store, or purchased online and shipped to a delivery address; they can be used at any till across the six participating brands. E-gift cards are delivered instantly by email or SMS as a digital code and QR image, redeemable online at checkout or by presenting the QR code at any store till.

The two formats carry identical value and redemption rules; the only functional difference is delivery mechanism and, for physical cards, the option to register the card to a Landmark Rewards account for balance protection (see Lost or Stolen Cards below). E-gift cards are always considered registered to the purchasing customer's account by default, since they are issued against an email or phone number at the time of purchase.

## Denominations Available

Physical gift cards are sold in-store and online in fixed denominations of AED 50, 100, 250, 500, and 1,000 (or the SAR/INR equivalent). E-gift cards purchased online support the same fixed denominations plus a custom amount option between AED 50 and AED 2,000 (SAR 50-2,000, INR 1,500-60,000). Corporate bulk gift card purchases (see below) are not bound by these individual denomination limits.

## Gift Cards vs. Promotional Vouchers: Expiry Rules

Gift cards purchased with real money (physical or e-gift) do not expire, in line with consumer protection regulations in the UAE and India that prohibit expiry dates on prepaid store-value instruments.

Promotional vouchers — codes issued free of charge by Landmark as part of a campaign, loyalty reward, birthday offer, or CX goodwill gesture — are a separate instrument from purchased gift cards and do carry an expiry date, which is always stated on the voucher itself or in the issuing email/SMS. Most promotional vouchers expire 30-90 days from issuance depending on the specific campaign; birthday vouchers under the Landmark Rewards program expire at the end of the member's birthday month. Expired promotional vouchers cannot be reactivated or extended under any circumstance, since they carry no monetary value paid by the customer.

## Redemption Rules

- Gift cards cannot be reloaded once the balance reaches zero; the customer must purchase a new card or e-gift card for further use.
- Partial redemption is allowed: if a purchase amount is less than the card balance, the remaining balance stays on the card (or is reflected in the e-gift card's remaining value) for future use, with no expiry as noted above.
- A single order can use more than one gift card, but the total gift card value applied cannot exceed the order subtotal; any shortfall must be covered by another payment method.
- Gift cards cannot be used to purchase additional gift cards, to prevent value-laundering between accounts.
- Gift cards cannot be combined with certain deep-discount clearance promotions that are explicitly marked "not valid with gift card redemption" on the campaign banner; this restriction does not apply to standard sale pricing, only to a small number of flagged clearance events.
- Landmark Rewards points can be redeemed alongside a gift card in the same transaction, since points and gift cards are treated as independent payment instruments.

## Lost or Stolen Gift Cards

Physical gift cards registered to a Landmark Rewards account at the time of purchase or activation can be frozen immediately upon report, with the remaining balance transferred to a replacement card within 3-5 business days, subject to a AED 10 (or local equivalent) replacement processing fee. Unregistered physical gift cards that are lost or stolen cannot be recovered, replaced, or refunded, since Landmark has no way to verify ownership or remaining balance without registration — customers are strongly encouraged to register any physical card to their account at the time of purchase.

E-gift cards, being tied to an email or phone number by default, are automatically protected: if a customer suspects their e-gift card code has been compromised before use, CX can deactivate the original code and issue a replacement to the verified account holder within 1-2 business days, provided the balance has not already been redeemed.

## Corporate and Bulk Gift Card Purchases

Businesses purchasing gift cards for employee rewards, client gifting, or event giveaways can place a corporate bulk order through the Landmark Group corporate sales desk, available in UAE, KSA, and India. Corporate orders require a minimum purchase of 50 cards or AED 5,000 in total value, whichever threshold is reached first, and support custom denominations outside the standard consumer range, up to AED 5,000 per card. Corporate orders are invoiced directly to the business (rather than paid at a till or standard checkout) and typically take 5-7 business days to fulfill for physical card orders, or 1-2 business days for bulk e-gift card issuance, given the need for batch code generation and quality checks.
