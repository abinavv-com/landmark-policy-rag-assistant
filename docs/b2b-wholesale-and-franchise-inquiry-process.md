# B2B Wholesale and Franchise Inquiry Process

This document covers the process for a business inquiring about becoming a Landmark Group **franchise partner** or **wholesale distribution partner** in a new market, or expanding an existing partner relationship into a new territory. It is entirely separate from the Corporate and Bulk Orders Policy, which governs businesses and institutions purchasing retail product in bulk quantities for their own use or gifting; this document instead covers businesses that want to operate a Landmark brand's stores or distribute Landmark brand products under a formal commercial agreement, rather than simply buying product as a customer.

## Who This Process Is For

Landmark Group brands expand into new geographies both through company-owned stores and through franchise or master-distribution partnerships with qualified regional operators, depending on the market and brand. This process applies to three categories of inquiry: prospective **franchisees** seeking to operate physical Lifestyle, Max, Splash, Home Centre, Centrepoint, or Babyshop stores under license in a market or region not currently served by a company-owned store; prospective **wholesale distribution partners** seeking to distribute Landmark private-label or brand-owned product ranges through their own retail or B2B channels in a new market; and existing partners in one market or brand seeking to expand their agreement into an additional territory or brand.

This is a business development and international expansion process, not a customer service function, and inquiries are handled by Landmark's International Business Development team rather than the standard CX or corporate sales channels used for bulk retail orders.

## Inquiry Channel and Initial Screening

Prospective partners submit an inquiry through the "Partner With Us" form on the Landmark Group corporate website, providing company registration details, relevant retail or distribution experience, the target market or region, proposed brand(s) of interest, and an outline of available capital and existing retail or logistics infrastructure. Landmark's International Business Development team screens every inquiry within **15 business days** to assess basic fit: whether the target market aligns with the brand's expansion strategy, whether the applicant's stated experience and capital profile meet the baseline threshold for the brand in question, and whether the market is already served by an existing partner or company-owned operation, which would typically preclude a new franchise award in the same territory.

Inquiries that do not pass initial screening receive a notification explaining the reason (market not currently open for expansion, capital profile below threshold, or insufficient retail experience) and are not carried forward, though an applicant may reapply after 12 months if their circumstances materially change.

## Minimum Capital and Experience Requirements

At a high level, minimum capital requirements vary by brand format and market, reflecting differences in store size, fit-out cost, and inventory intensity across the portfolio: value-fashion formats such as Max and Splash carry a lower entry capital threshold than large-format destination brands such as Home Centre, which requires substantially higher capital given furniture and homeware inventory carrying costs and larger store footprints. Prospective partners should expect Landmark to require, at minimum, demonstrated access to sufficient working capital to fund store fit-out, an opening inventory position, and at least 12-18 months of operating runway before the store or distribution operation is expected to reach profitability, in addition to prior retail or distribution operating experience in the target market. Landmark's Business Development team provides brand-specific and market-specific capital guidance directly to applicants who pass initial screening, since publishing exact figures externally is not standard practice given how much they vary by market conditions and store format.

## Due Diligence and Agreement Negotiation

Applicants who pass initial screening move into a due diligence phase, typically taking **6-10 weeks**, covering financial verification, site or territory assessment (for franchise applicants, this includes evaluating proposed store locations against Landmark's format and catchment criteria), and a reference check against the applicant's prior retail or distribution track record. Applicants who clear due diligence enter commercial negotiation of the franchise or distribution agreement, covering territory exclusivity, royalty or margin structure, minimum purchase or sales commitments, brand standards compliance requirements, and term length, which typically takes a further **6-12 weeks** depending on the complexity of the territory and brand involved.

## Timeline From Inquiry to Signed Agreement

Taken together, the process from initial inquiry submission to a signed franchise or distribution agreement typically spans **4 to 9 months**, varying primarily based on how quickly the applicant can produce financial documentation, how straightforward the proposed territory and site selection is, and how much negotiation the commercial terms require. Store opening or first product shipment then follows the signed agreement by a further period set out in the agreement itself, commonly 4-8 months for a franchise store build-out, and considerably shorter for a wholesale distribution arrangement that does not require physical retail construction.

## Ongoing Partner Support

Once an agreement is signed, franchise and wholesale partners are assigned a dedicated International Business Development relationship manager, distinct from the domestic B2B account managers who handle bulk retail accounts under the Corporate and Bulk Orders Policy, who coordinates brand standards training, initial and ongoing merchandising support, and supply chain onboarding for the new territory. Renewal terms, performance review cadence, and expansion rights into further territories are set out in the individual partnership agreement and vary by brand and region.
