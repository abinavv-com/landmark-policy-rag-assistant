# Click-and-Collect FAQ

This document expands on the click-and-collect basics covered in the Store and Online Service FAQ, with detail on store eligibility, pickup windows, ID verification, and partial-availability handling across Lifestyle, Max, Splash, Home Centre, Centrepoint, and Babyshop.

## Which Stores Support Click-and-Collect

Click-and-collect is available at all Lifestyle, Max, Splash, and Centrepoint stores across the UAE, KSA, and India. Home Centre supports click-and-collect only at large-format stores with dedicated stockroom capacity; smaller Home Centre express-format locations inside community malls do not offer this service, since they lack the backroom space to hold reserved orders alongside regular floor stock. Babyshop supports click-and-collect at all standalone stores but not at in-mall shop-in-shop counters, which operate on consignment stock managed by the host mall's inventory system rather than Babyshop's own warehouse feed.

The product page and checkout flow only display a "Collect in Store" option for stores that genuinely support the service for that specific item, based on real-time stock availability at that location, so customers will not see the option for an ineligible store or an out-of-stock SKU.

## Pickup Window Before Auto-Cancellation

Once an order is confirmed ready for collection, it is held at the selected store for 5 calendar days from the ready-for-pickup notification. This window is deliberately shorter than a typical delivery-return window because held stock is physically reserved and cannot be sold to walk-in customers or reallocated to other online orders during that period.

If the order is not collected within the 5-day window, it is automatically cancelled and the stock is restocked to the shop floor. The refund for an auto-cancelled order is issued to the original payment method within 3-5 business days, matching the standard refund timeline used elsewhere for cancelled online orders. Customers who anticipate needing more time should contact the store directly before the window lapses; store teams can extend the hold by up to 3 additional days at their discretion if the customer confirms they are still coming, though this extension is not guaranteed and is not available through the app or website.

## ID Verification Requirements

To collect an order, the customer must present the digital or printed order confirmation along with a valid government-issued photo ID (Emirates ID, Iqama, Aadhaar, passport, or driving license, depending on market) matching the name on the order. As an alternative to a physical ID, the customer can present their Landmark Rewards membership card or the logged-in app profile linked to the order, since the account itself has already been identity-verified at enrollment.

Store staff cross-check the name on the ID or Rewards profile against the name on the order confirmation before releasing the item. A mismatch results in the order being held rather than released, pending either a collection-on-behalf authorization (see below) or the account holder collecting in person.

## Collecting on Someone Else's Behalf

Customers can nominate an alternate collector at the time of placing the order, or by contacting CX before the pickup window closes, by providing the alternate collector's full name and ID type in advance. The nominated collector must present their own valid ID matching the name provided in the authorization, along with the order confirmation.

Walk-in collection by someone not pre-authorized on the order is not permitted, even if that person has the order confirmation number or a screenshot of the confirmation email, since the confirmation alone does not establish identity. This rule exists primarily to prevent pickup fraud on higher-value electronics and jewelry orders at Lifestyle and Home Centre, where such attempts are more commonly flagged.

## Partial Availability on Multi-Item Orders

When an order contains items from different stock pools or suppliers, it is common for some items to be ready for collection before others. In this case, the customer receives a notification for the portion that is ready and can choose to collect that portion immediately or wait for the full order to consolidate.

If the remaining items are delayed beyond 3 business days past the original estimated ready date, CX proactively reaches out with the option to either continue waiting, convert the delayed portion to home delivery at no extra cost, or cancel the delayed portion for a refund while keeping the already-collected items. The 5-day collection window for the first-ready portion still applies independently and is not extended simply because a companion item in the same order is delayed.
