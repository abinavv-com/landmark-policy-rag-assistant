# Multi-Currency and Cross-Border Loyalty FAQ

This document explains how a single Landmark Rewards account behaves when a member shops across the UAE, KSA, and India, how points are valued across the three local currencies (AED, SAR, INR), and how redemption works when traveling to a different Landmark market than where the points were earned.

## One Account, Three Currencies

A Landmark Rewards account is not tied to a single country; a member who registers in one market can shop in any of the other two markets using the same mobile number or email login, and all purchases across all three markets pool into the same unified points balance, in addition to pooling across all six brands as described in the Loyalty Program FAQ. This means a customer who lives in Dubai but travels frequently to Mumbai or Riyadh, for instance, does not need to hold separate accounts per country and does not lose or fragment their points history when shopping in a different market.

## How Points Are Calculated Across Currencies

Points accrue at the same underlying rate in every market: 1 point per AED 10, SAR 10, or INR 100 spent, applied directly to the transaction in whichever local currency the purchase was made, without any cross-currency conversion at the point of earning. In practical terms, this means the point-earning rate is calibrated per market to reflect each currency's relative value (AED and SAR are pegged close to parity with each other, while INR 100 reflects a materially different absolute spend threshold), rather than points being calculated by first converting every purchase into a single reference currency. A customer does not need to think about exchange rates when earning points in any of the three markets — the local rate simply applies to the local transaction.

## How Points Are Valued at Redemption

Redemption value is also fixed per local currency rather than floating with exchange rates: 100 points redeem for AED 10 of purchase value in the UAE, SAR 10 in KSA, or INR 100 in India, applied as a discount in whichever market the redemption is taking place, regardless of which market the points were originally earned in. The same 100-point balance therefore carries a different effective value depending on where it is redeemed (SAR and AED values are close to equivalent, while the INR value reflects the same points-to-currency-unit ratio applied to India's local pricing); Landmark does not offer a way to convert a points balance into a fixed currency-equivalent value that travels unchanged between markets.

## Redemption While Traveling to a Different Market

A member with points earned primarily in one market can redeem their full pooled balance in any of the other two markets without restriction or penalty, since there is no rule requiring points to be redeemed in the market where they were earned. For example, a customer who earned the bulk of their points shopping at Lifestyle and Home Centre in the UAE can redeem that same balance at a Centrepoint or Max store while visiting Riyadh, or at a Lifestyle store while visiting Mumbai, using the standard local redemption rate applicable at the time of the transaction.

Because the underlying earning and redemption rates are set locally per market rather than through a live currency-conversion engine, a member's effective purchasing power from their points balance can vary modestly between markets. CX agents should not describe this as a "loss" of value when a customer redeems outside the market where they earned the points, since no conversion fee or penalty is applied; the ratio is simply the standard local redemption rate for the market the customer happens to be shopping in.

## Tier Status Is Also Unified Across Markets

Silver, Gold, and Platinum tier status is calculated on a single rolling 12-month spend figure that combines purchases from all three markets and all six brands into one number, using each purchase's local-currency amount without a currency-conversion step to a common reference currency; tier thresholds are separately defined per currency (AED 3,000 for Gold, and the corresponding SAR/INR figures), and spend in each market counts toward the threshold framework for that market rather than a blended cross-currency total. In practice, the large majority of members shop predominantly within a single home market, and cross-border spend is treated as additive to that pattern for tier purposes.

## Account Display and Redemption Failures

A member's Rewards tab displays their points balance as a single number (points are currency-agnostic as a unit), along with a breakdown of recent transactions showing the local currency and amount for each purchase that contributed points. There is no separate "home market" setting to configure; the system automatically reflects whichever market's storefront or till the member is transacting through.

If a member attempts to redeem points at a till experiencing a temporary connectivity issue with the central Landmark Rewards ledger (rare, occasionally affecting newly-opened franchise-partner locations), the transaction can still be completed as a standard cash or card sale without the points discount applied, and the member can request the points value be credited back as a goodwill voucher through CX once the issue is resolved, following the escalation path in the Complaint Escalation Process.
