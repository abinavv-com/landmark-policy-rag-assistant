# Size and Fit Guide

Landmark Group brands each maintain their own size charts, and while we use shared labels (S, M, L, XL or numeric sizes), the actual garment measurements behind those labels are not identical across brands. This guide explains how to use brand-specific size charts and flags the most common cross-brand fit discrepancies our customers encounter.

## Why Sizes Differ Across Brands

Max and Splash, both positioned as value-fashion brands with younger, trend-driven customer bases, use a size chart that runs approximately half a size to a full size smaller than Lifestyle's chart for the same labeled size. For example, a women's Medium at Lifestyle typically corresponds to a 38-39 cm chest measurement range, while a women's Medium at Max corresponds to roughly 36-37 cm. This is a known and consistent pain point raised in customer feedback, particularly for customers who shop across both Max and Lifestyle expecting identical fit from the same label.

Splash's chart is closely aligned with Max's rather than Lifestyle's, since both brands source from overlapping supplier bases in the value-fashion segment. Centrepoint, which houses both Lifestyle-aligned and Max-aligned sub-brands under one roof, does not have a single unified size chart; customers should check the specific sub-brand's size tab on each product page rather than assuming a Centrepoint-wide standard.

Home Centre's sizing applies only to soft furnishings (bedsheets, curtains, mattress protectors) and follows regional standard dimensions (UK/US bed sizes for the India market, Euro/UAE standard sizes for Gulf markets), not apparel-style lettered sizes.

## How to Use the Size Guide

1. Navigate to the product page and select the "Size Guide" tab, which is brand-specific and pulls the size chart for that exact brand and category (e.g., Women's Tops, Men's Trousers, Kids' Footwear).
2. Locate your body measurement (bust/chest, waist, hip, or inseam in cm) in the left-hand column and match it to the corresponding labeled size in that brand's chart.
3. Do not carry over a size you know from a different brand. A Lifestyle Medium and a Max Medium are not the same garment dimensions, even though both display "M" on the label.
4. For footwear, always check whether the size is listed in EU, UK, or US convention, since Lifestyle predominantly lists EU sizing while Max and Splash sometimes list UK sizing for imported footwear ranges.

## Brand-Specific Fit Notes

**Max**: Cuts run slightly smaller than Lifestyle in the same labeled size, most noticeably in the shoulder and bust measurements for women's tops and the waist measurement for men's trousers. Customers moving from Lifestyle to Max are generally advised to size up by one size if between sizes.

**Splash**: Similar to Max, Splash's fit skews smaller than Lifestyle, with particularly narrow shoulder cuts on its formal shirting range. Splash's denim range fits true to its own chart but runs smaller than Lifestyle's denim.

**Lifestyle**: Runs closest to a traditional international size standard among Landmark's fashion brands, with the most generous cut in the value-to-premium range.

**Centrepoint (kids' categories)**: Kids' sizing is based on age-range labeling (e.g., 4-5Y, 6-7Y) cross-referenced with height in cm, since children's body proportions vary more by height than a single age label can capture. Parents are encouraged to measure the child's height and match to the height column rather than relying on age alone.

**Babyshop**: Infant and toddler clothing sizing is based on both age range and weight in kg, since weight is a more reliable fit indicator than age for garments in the 0-24 month range. The size chart lists a weight bracket alongside each age-range label.

## When in Doubt

If a customer is between two sizes or shopping across brands for a matching outfit (e.g., a Lifestyle top with Max trousers), CX agents should recommend ordering both adjacent sizes where free returns apply and keeping the better fit, rather than guessing from a single brand's chart applied to another brand.
