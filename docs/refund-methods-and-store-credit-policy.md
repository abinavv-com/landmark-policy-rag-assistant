# Refund Methods and Store Credit Policy

This document sets out the mechanics of how a refund is issued once a return, cancellation, or approved claim has been processed, across all Landmark Group brands (Lifestyle, Max, Splash, Home Centre, Centrepoint, Babyshop) in the UAE, KSA, and India. It is a companion to the Returns and Exchange Policy and the Payment Methods and Billing FAQ; this document focuses on the choice between original-method refund, store credit, and exchange, and the timing and incentives attached to each.

## The Three Refund Outcomes

When a return or approved claim is finalized, the customer is offered one of three outcomes, selectable at the time of return initiation (online) or at the returns counter (in-store):

1. **Refund to original payment method.** The value is returned to the card, wallet, UPI account, or BNPL provider used for the original purchase. This is the default outcome if the customer does not actively choose otherwise, and it is the only option for customers who prefer not to shop again with Landmark in the near term.
2. **Store credit.** The value is issued as a Landmark-wide digital credit, usable across all six brands, in place of a cash-equivalent refund. Store credit is the fastest-settling option and carries a bonus incentive, described below.
3. **Exchange.** Rather than a refund, the customer receives a different size, color, or item of equal value in the same transaction, avoiding a separate refund-and-repurchase cycle entirely. Exchanges are treated as a direct swap and do not touch the refund timelines described in this document, since no money changes hands.

Store credit and original-method refund are mutually exclusive per return; a customer cannot split a single returned item's value between the two.

## Store Credit Bonus Incentive

Customers who opt for store credit instead of an original-method cash refund receive a **10% bonus on the returned value**, credited to their Landmark Rewards wallet balance instantly upon return approval. For example, a AED 500 return refunded as store credit is issued as AED 550 in usable credit. This incentive exists because store credit keeps value within the Landmark ecosystem and avoids the settlement delay and processing cost associated with reversing a card or bank transaction, so CX agents are trained to mention the bonus as an option whenever a customer initiates a return, without pressuring the customer to choose it over a cash refund.

The 10% bonus applies uniformly across all six brands and is not affected by the customer's Landmark Rewards tier; Silver, Gold, and Platinum members all receive the same bonus rate on store credit conversions.

## Store Credit Expiry

Store credit issued through a return or approved claim is valid for **12 months from the date of issuance**. Unlike purchased gift cards, which do not expire under UAE and India consumer-protection rules on prepaid store value, return-derived store credit is treated as a goodwill-adjacent instrument tied to the original transaction and does carry the 12-month expiry. Customers receive an app or email reminder 30 days before any store credit balance is due to expire, mirroring the Landmark Rewards points expiry notification pattern. Expired store credit cannot be reactivated, extended, or converted back to a cash refund.

## Refund Timing by Payment Method

Timing to the customer's account or statement depends on the original payment method, consistent with the figures set out in the Payment Methods and Billing FAQ:

- **Cards (Visa/Mastercard/Amex) and Apple Pay**: 5-7 business days.
- **UPI, net banking, and mobile wallets (India)**: 3-5 business days.
- **Tabby / Tamara BNPL**: typically 5-7 business days, processed through the BNPL provider rather than directly by Landmark.
- **Cash on delivery**: refunded via bank transfer, requiring the customer to submit IBAN (UAE/KSA) or bank account and IFSC details (India); this takes 7-10 business days to settle, the longest of any method.
- **Store credit**: issued instantly, with no processing delay, which is the primary practical advantage of choosing this option over an original-method refund.
- **Landmark Rewards points or gift card used as full or partial payment**: the points or gift card portion is restored to the customer's account instantly, with only the remaining cash portion, if any, following the method-based timelines above.

## Partial Refunds and Multi-Item Orders

When only some items in a multi-item order are returned, the refund is calculated on the returned items' post-discount value only, and a proportional shipping fee refund follows the same rules as a full-order refund only if all items are returned. If an order was paid using a mix of a gift card, Landmark Rewards points, and a card, the refund is unwound in reverse order of application: card portion first, then points, then gift card balance, so the customer's non-expiring instruments are restored last.

## When Store Credit Is Mandatory

In a small number of cases, store credit is issued as the only refund outcome rather than a customer choice: when the original payment method has been closed, expired, or is otherwise unable to receive a reversal (for example, a canceled card), and when a promotional voucher, rather than cash, funded part or all of the original purchase. In these cases, the 10% bonus still applies, and CX communicates clearly to the customer why a cash refund is not available for that specific order.
