# Data Privacy and Account Security FAQ

This document covers how Landmark Group collects, retains, and protects customer data across its online and in-store systems in the UAE, KSA, and India, spanning Lifestyle, Max, Splash, Home Centre, Centrepoint, and Babyshop, along with account security options and how to exercise data rights.

## What Data Is Collected and Why

Landmark collects account and contact data (name, email, phone number, date of birth for age-restricted purchases and birthday-month loyalty perks), transaction data (order history, payment method type — not full card numbers, which are tokenized by the payment processor — and returns/exchange history), delivery data (shipping addresses, delivery preferences), and app/website usage data (browsing and search behavior, device identifiers, and approximate location derived from IP address) used to power product recommendations, fraud detection, and Landmark Rewards points calculation. Biometric or precise GPS location data is not collected; store visit tracking, where used for footfall analytics, relies on anonymized Wi-Fi/Bluetooth beacon signals rather than identifying individual customers.

Data is used for order fulfillment, customer service, fraud and payment-risk screening, loyalty program administration, and — where the customer has opted in — marketing communications and personalized product recommendations. Data is not sold to third parties; it may be shared with processing partners strictly necessary to fulfill a transaction (payment gateways, delivery couriers, the student/staff discount verification vendor, and BNPL providers), each bound by a data processing agreement limiting their use of the data to the specific service provided.

## Data Retention Periods

Account and transaction data is retained for as long as a customer maintains an active account, plus 7 years following account closure or the customer's last transaction, in order to meet UAE, KSA, and Indian financial and tax record-keeping requirements. Marketing preference and consent records are retained for 3 years from the date of last consent activity, after which a lapsed, non-engaged marketing profile is automatically purged. App usage and browsing behavior data used for personalization is retained on a rolling 24-month basis, with data older than 24 months automatically aged out of the active recommendation engine.

## Requesting Account Data Deletion

Although the UAE, KSA, and India do not currently impose a GDPR-equivalent statutory deletion right on Landmark, the company extends GDPR-style data subject rights to all customers regardless of region as a matter of policy: any customer can request a copy of their held personal data, a correction of inaccurate data, or full account and data deletion by submitting a request through the "Privacy Requests" section of the Landmark Rewards app or by emailing the designated privacy contact listed in the app's privacy settings. Verified deletion requests are processed within 30 calendar days. Deletion removes marketing, browsing, and personalization data outright; transaction records tied to completed orders are retained in a de-identified or minimally identifying form for the statutory 7-year financial record period even after account deletion, since Landmark cannot lawfully erase records required for tax and audit compliance, but the account itself, saved addresses, and browsing profile are permanently removed.

Landmark Rewards points and any active Landmark+ membership are forfeited upon account deletion and are not refunded or transferable, since deletion is treated as a full account closure.

## Two-Factor Authentication

Customers can enable two-factor authentication (2FA) on their Landmark Rewards account from the account security settings, choosing between an SMS one-time password sent to the registered mobile number or an authenticator-app-based time-based code (compatible with any standard TOTP app). Once enabled, 2FA is required for login from a new or unrecognized device, and for any change to the account's registered email, phone number, or linked payment method, regardless of which 2FA method is chosen. 2FA is optional but strongly recommended by the CX team for any account with a saved payment method or an active Landmark+ subscription.

## Reporting a Compromised Account

A customer who suspects their account has been compromised (unrecognized orders, a changed password they did not initiate, or a login notification from an unfamiliar device or location) should immediately use the "Secure My Account" option in the app, which force-logs-out all active sessions and requires a password reset before any further access. If unauthorized orders have already been placed, the customer should report this to CX through the priority security queue (available to all customers, not only Gold/Platinum tier members) so the orders can be flagged, cancellation attempted before dispatch where possible, and any fraudulent charge disputed with the payment processor. Confirmed account compromise cases are logged and monitored for 90 days for recurring suspicious activity.

## Managing Cookie and Tracking Preferences

On first visit, customers are presented with a cookie consent banner allowing them to accept all cookies, reject all non-essential cookies, or customize preferences by category (strictly necessary, performance/analytics, and marketing/advertising cookies). Preferences can be revisited and changed at any time via the "Cookie Preferences" link in the website footer or the equivalent setting in the mobile app's privacy menu. Rejecting performance or marketing cookies does not affect the ability to browse, purchase, or use Landmark Rewards, though it may reduce the relevance of on-site product recommendations and disable retargeted advertising on third-party platforms.
