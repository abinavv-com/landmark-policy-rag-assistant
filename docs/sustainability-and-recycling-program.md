# Sustainability and Recycling Program

This document covers Landmark Group's textile take-back program, packaging sustainability commitments, sustainable product labeling, and e-waste recycling for appliance purchases, spanning Lifestyle, Max, Splash, Home Centre, Centrepoint, and Babyshop across the UAE, KSA, and India.

## In-Store Textile Take-Back Program

Customers can drop off used clothing and textiles (from any brand, not only Landmark Group brands) at designated take-back bins located at all Lifestyle, Max, Splash, and Centrepoint stores, and at select larger-format Home Centre stores that stock a home textiles range. There is no minimum or maximum number of items per drop-off, and items do not need to be Landmark-purchased or in resellable condition — the program accepts worn, stained, or damaged textiles, since collected material is routed to textile recycling or downcycling partners rather than resale.

As a participation incentive, customers who drop off at least 3 items of clothing or textiles receive a discount voucher worth AED 25 (or SAR 25 / INR 500) off their next purchase of AED 150 or more at the same brand, issued instantly at the till or via SMS for online-linked accounts. This voucher expires 60 days from issuance and follows standard promotional voucher rules rather than gift card rules, meaning it is not extendable once expired. A single Landmark Rewards account can claim this take-back voucher a maximum of once per calendar month per brand, to prevent repeat-visit abuse of the incentive.

## Packaging Sustainability Commitments

Online orders across all six brands are shipped in recyclable poly mailers or corrugated cardboard boxes made from a minimum of 80% recycled content, phased in progressively across regional fulfillment centers. Home Centre furniture and large-format deliveries use reusable protective blankets and corner guards for last-mile delivery in UAE metro areas, collected back by the delivery team at the point of drop-off rather than left as single-use packaging waste.

Landmark has committed to eliminating single-use plastic garment bags from in-store point-of-sale packaging across Lifestyle, Max, Splash, and Centrepoint stores in the UAE and KSA, replacing them with paper carry bags or reusable fabric totes available for a nominal AED 5 charge. This transition is being rolled out market by market and is not yet complete across all India store formats as of this document's publication, where a hybrid paper/plastic transition period remains in effect at smaller-format stores.

## Sustainable Product Line Labeling

Products meeting Landmark's internal sustainability criteria — such as organic cotton content above 50%, recycled polyester content above 30%, or certified responsible sourcing (e.g., better cotton or FSC-certified wood for Home Centre furniture) — carry a green leaf hangtag and a corresponding "Conscious Choice" filter tag on the product listing page online. This labeling is applied at the product level, not the brand level, meaning a single brand's catalog will show a mix of labeled and unlabeled items depending on each specific product's sourcing. Customers can filter by "Conscious Choice" within the brand's online storefront to browse only labeled items within a category.

Labeling claims are reviewed by Landmark's sourcing compliance team against supplier certification documentation before a product is approved to carry the hangtag, and mislabeled products identified after listing are corrected and, where the discrepancy is material, flagged for supplier review.

## E-Waste Recycling for Home Centre Appliances

Customers purchasing a major appliance from Home Centre (refrigerators, washing machines, air conditioners, and similar large electronics) can request free e-waste collection of their old appliance at the time of new delivery, provided the request is made when placing the order or at least 24 hours before the scheduled delivery slot. The old appliance is collected by the same delivery team and routed to a certified e-waste recycler rather than landfill disposal, in line with regional e-waste handling regulations in the UAE and India.

For small appliances (kettles, blenders, irons, and similar countertop electronics) that are not part of a new delivery, customers can instead drop off the old item at any Home Centre store's e-waste collection point, with no purchase requirement attached to the drop-off. Both collection routes are available only for e-waste, not general household waste or non-electronic items, and Home Centre does not currently offer e-waste collection in Saudi Arabia due to differences in local recycling infrastructure partnerships; this route is available only in the UAE and India at present.

## Program Reporting and Limitations

Landmark publishes an annual sustainability progress update summarizing tonnage collected through the textile take-back program and e-waste channel, alongside packaging recycled-content percentages achieved that year. These programs are operationally distinct from the standard returns process described in the Returns and Exchange Policy: items dropped off for take-back or e-waste recycling are not eligible for any refund or exchange value, since they are donations to the recycling program rather than returns of a Landmark purchase.
