# Warranty Policy

Warranty coverage across Landmark Group brands varies significantly by product category. This document sets out warranty terms, exclusions, and the claims process for Home Centre, Lifestyle, Max, Splash, Centrepoint, and Babyshop.

## Warranty Terms by Category

### Electronics and Appliances (Home Centre)

Home Centre's small appliances (kettles, blenders, air fryers, vacuum cleaners) carry a manufacturer warranty of 12 months from the invoice date, covering defects in materials and workmanship. Major appliances sold through Home Centre's furniture and homeware lines, such as refrigerators and washing machines from partner brands, carry the original manufacturer's warranty, typically 24 months, administered directly by the manufacturer's regional service center rather than Home Centre's own CX team.

Furniture items (sofas, dining sets, storage units) carry a 24-month structural warranty against manufacturing defects in frames, mechanisms (recliners, sofa beds), and upholstery seams, but explicitly exclude fabric fading from sunlight exposure and normal wear marks on high-traffic upholstery.

### Footwear (all brands)

Footwear sold at Lifestyle, Max, Splash, and Centrepoint carries a 90-day warranty against manufacturing defects such as sole separation, stitching failure, or buckle/zipper malfunction occurring under normal use. This is materially shorter than the appliance warranty window and is a frequent source of customer confusion, since customers sometimes expect footwear to carry the same coverage length as electronics.

### Apparel (all brands)

Apparel items do not carry a traditional "warranty" in the manufacturing-defect sense beyond the standard 30-day return window described in the Returns and Exchange Policy. However, Lifestyle's premium formalwear line and Centrepoint's school uniform range both offer a 60-day defect guarantee covering issues like seam failure or color bleeding on first wash, provided care label instructions were followed.

### Watches and Jewelry (Lifestyle, Centrepoint)

Watches sold at Lifestyle carry the brand's own international warranty card, typically 24 months, honored through Lifestyle's authorized service counters. Fashion jewelry (non-precious metal) carries a 30-day defect warranty only, covering plating loss or clasp failure, and explicitly excludes tarnishing from perfume, sweat, or water exposure, which is considered normal wear rather than a defect.

### Baby Gear (Babyshop)

Babyshop's hard goods category — strollers, car seats, cribs — carries a 12-month warranty on frame and mechanical components (wheels, folding mechanisms, harness buckles) but excludes fabric and foam padding wear after 6 months of documented use, given the accelerated wear cycle typical of baby products.

## What's Covered vs. Excluded

Covered under all warranty categories:

- Manufacturing defects present at the time of purchase or arising from normal, intended use.
- Component failure (zippers, buttons, mechanical parts) not caused by external damage.
- Electrical faults in appliances not caused by voltage mismatch or unauthorized repair.

Excluded from all warranty categories:

- Damage from misuse, accident, or unauthorized repair/modification.
- Normal wear and tear, including sole wear, fabric pilling, and color fading from washing or sun exposure.
- Damage from exposure to water, extreme heat, or chemicals beyond intended use.
- Products purchased from unauthorized resellers or secondary marketplaces.
- Cosmetic scratches or dents that do not affect product function, reported after 7 days of delivery.

## Claim Process

To initiate a warranty claim, the customer should visit any store of the purchasing brand with the original invoice or order confirmation, or raise a claim through the "My Orders" section of the brand's app or website for online purchases. The in-store team or online CX agent inspects the item (or reviews submitted photos for online claims) and classifies the issue as a covered defect or an exclusion within 3 business days.

Approved claims result in one of three outcomes at Landmark's discretion: free repair, like-for-like replacement, or store credit equal to the original purchase price. Repairs for appliances routed to a manufacturer service center may take 10-15 business days depending on parts availability. Claims lacking a valid invoice can still be processed if the purchase can be verified against the customer's Landmark Rewards account, since all rewards-linked purchases are logged centrally.
