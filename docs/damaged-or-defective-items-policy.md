# Damaged or Defective Items Policy

This policy covers items that arrive damaged, incomplete, or visibly defective at the point of delivery, across all Landmark Group brands (Lifestyle, Max, Splash, Home Centre, Centrepoint, Babyshop) in the UAE, KSA, and India. It is distinct from both the standard Returns and Exchange Policy (which covers change-of-mind returns) and the Warranty Policy (which covers failures that develop after a period of normal use).

## Reporting Window

Damage-in-transit or defect-on-arrival claims must be reported within 48 hours of the delivery timestamp, a much shorter window than the standard 30-day return period, because prompt reporting is what allows Landmark to distinguish carrier damage from customer-caused damage and to pursue recovery from the courier partner where applicable. Claims submitted after 48 hours are redirected to the standard returns process if the item still qualifies as unused and within the brand's return window, or to the Warranty Policy claims process if the issue is more consistent with a manufacturing defect than shipping damage.

For furniture and large appliances delivered by a two-person white-glove crew (a Home Centre-specific delivery model), the reporting window is extended to 72 hours, since these items are often not fully unpacked and assembled until a day or two after delivery.

## Required Evidence

To process a damage or defect claim, the customer must submit clear photos showing:

- The damaged or defective item itself, with the fault clearly visible.
- The outer shipping box or packaging, including the shipping label, showing any visible external damage.
- The product's original barcode or tag, confirming it is the item ordered.

Landmark strongly recommends that customers record a short unboxing video for higher-value items (electronics, furniture, jewelry, and watches), since a video provides stronger evidence than static photos and typically results in faster claim approval, though a video is not a strict requirement for the claim to be accepted. Claims lacking sufficient photo evidence are not rejected outright; CX will request additional images before a decision is made, which does add 1-2 business days to resolution time.

## Replacement vs. Refund Choice

Once a damage or defect claim is approved, the customer chooses between a like-for-like replacement (subject to stock availability) or a full refund to the original payment method. If the exact item is out of stock, CX will offer the refund option along with a 10% store credit bonus (matching the incentive structure used for standard return store credits) as an alternative to a card refund, which can take longer to settle.

There is no restocking fee, inspection fee, or any deduction from the refund or replacement for a confirmed damage-in-transit or defect-on-arrival claim, since the fault is not attributable to the customer.

## Expedited Replacement Shipping

Approved replacement orders for damaged or defective items are shipped via expedited courier at no cost to the customer, regardless of the customer's Landmark Rewards tier or the original order's shipping method. In UAE metro areas, expedited replacement typically arrives within 1-2 business days; in KSA and India, the expedited timeline is 2-4 business days, still faster than the standard delivery window for a new order in those markets. Customers are never asked to pay the price difference if the replacement item's current price is higher than at the time of original purchase.

## How This Differs From a Warranty Claim

The distinction between this policy and the Warranty Policy is time of discovery, not severity of the fault:

- **Damaged or defective on arrival** (this policy): the fault is present at, or caused by, delivery — a cracked appliance panel, a torn garment seam noticed on unboxing, a scratched watch face straight out of the box. Reported within 48 hours (72 hours for white-glove furniture delivery), handled via photo/video evidence and expedited replacement at no cost.
- **Warranty claim** (see Warranty Policy): the fault develops after a period of normal use — a stroller wheel that fails after three months, a kettle that stops heating after eight months. Handled through the brand's warranty claims desk, subject to the category-specific warranty terms (for example, 12 months for Home Centre small appliances, 90 days for footwear), and inspected in-store or via submitted photos before a repair, replacement, or store credit decision.

CX agents should ask the customer when the issue was first noticed relative to delivery date before routing the case, since the two processes have different evidence requirements, approval timelines, and cost outcomes.
