# Seasonal Workforce and Festive Staffing FAQ (Internal)

**Audience note**: This document is for internal use by store operations managers, HR business partners, and seasonal/temporary retail staff. It is not customer-facing and should not be referenced in customer-support responses. It covers onboarding, discount eligibility, scheduling, and conversion pathways for temporary staff hired ahead of Landmark Group's peak trading periods — End of Season Sale (EOSS), Ramadan, and the year-end festive season — across Lifestyle, Max, Splash, Home Centre, Centrepoint, and Babyshop in the UAE, KSA, and India.

## Why Seasonal Staffing Exists

Landmark's peak trading periods generate footfall and transaction volume several multiples above baseline, particularly during EOSS markdown weekends, the final 10 days of Ramadan, and the four weeks leading into year-end festive gifting season. Rather than permanently overstaffing stores for a small number of peak weeks, each brand's operations team forecasts peak-period headcount needs by store and hires temporary seasonal staff on fixed-term contracts to cover the gap, supplementing the permanent floor team rather than replacing it.

## Onboarding Timeline

Seasonal hiring and onboarding is planned on a fixed lead-time calendar ahead of each peak period:

- **EOSS (twice yearly, per market calendar)**: seasonal recruitment opens 5 weeks before the sale start date, with onboarding (contract signing, uniform fitting, till and POS training) completed no later than 1 week before EOSS begins.
- **Ramadan**: recruitment opens 6 weeks ahead of the start of Ramadan to account for the compressed working-hours schedule and the need for staff briefed on Ramadan-specific customer service norms (adjusted greeting hours, gifting-focused merchandising); onboarding completes 2 weeks before Ramadan begins.
- **Year-end festive season**: recruitment opens 8 weeks ahead of the peak window (the final two weeks of December through the first week of January), the longest lead time of the three peaks, since this period also overlaps with the highest gift-wrapping and click-and-collect volumes described in the Festive and Gifting Services FAQ.

All seasonal staff complete a condensed 2-day onboarding program (versus the 5-day program for permanent new hires) covering till operations, the standard Returns and Exchange Policy at a working level, loyalty program enrollment steps, and brand-specific product knowledge, delivered by the store's training lead rather than the centralized new-hire academy used for permanent staff.

## Temporary Staff Discount Eligibility

Seasonal and temporary staff are eligible for a **10% employee discount**, half the 20% rate extended to confirmed permanent employees under the Employee Purchase and Referral Program, and it activates immediately upon contract signing rather than after a 90-day probation period, since most seasonal contracts run shorter than the standard probation window. The same category exclusions apply (sale items, gift cards, beauty-hall concessions, made-to-order goods), and the discount is capped at AED 1,000 per calendar month, half the permanent-employee cap, tracked against the seasonal worker's temporary HR ID. The discount deactivates automatically on the seasonal contract's stated end date unless the worker converts to a permanent role, in which case the rate and cap step up to the permanent-employee terms from the conversion date.

Seasonal staff are not eligible to participate in the Friends-and-Family Referral Program during their temporary contract term, since referral bonus payout requires an active, non-expiring HR ID; this restriction lifts automatically upon conversion to permanent status.

## Shift and Scheduling Policy During Peak Season

Seasonal staff schedules are published on a rolling 2-week basis rather than the standard 4-week schedule used for permanent staff, given how quickly peak-period footfall forecasts can shift based on weather, sale-day performance, and last-minute promotional pushes. Seasonal contracts specify a minimum guaranteed 20 hours per week and a maximum of 48 hours per week in line with regional labor law caps in the UAE, KSA, and India; any week where actual scheduled hours fall outside this band requires store manager sign-off and, in India, compliance review given stricter weekly-hours documentation requirements under the Shops and Establishments Act framework applicable in most states.

Store managers are required to schedule at least one experienced permanent staff member alongside seasonal staff on every shift during EOSS weekends and the final week before major festive dates, ensuring a trained escalation point is always present on the floor for complex returns, price-match, or complaint-escalation situations that fall outside a seasonal hire's 2-day training scope.

## Conversion Path to Permanent Role

Seasonal staff who complete their full contract term with a satisfactory performance review (assessed by the store manager against standard sales-floor and customer-service criteria) are eligible to apply for open permanent roles at the same store or, where no opening exists locally, at other stores within the same brand and market through an internal-priority application process that runs ahead of external recruitment for a 30-day window. Conversion is not automatic or guaranteed; it depends on store-level headcount budget and role availability at the time the seasonal contract ends, and seasonal staff are informed of this at the point of hire so expectations are set correctly from day one. Converted staff begin a fresh 90-day probationary period as a permanent employee, consistent with standard HR policy, after which the full 20% employee discount and referral program eligibility activate.
