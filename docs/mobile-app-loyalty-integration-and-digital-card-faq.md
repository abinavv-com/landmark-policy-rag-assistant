# Mobile App Loyalty Integration and Digital Card FAQ

This document covers how the Landmark Rewards digital membership card works inside the Landmark apps, the in-store scan-to-earn process, retroactive point crediting when a customer forgets to scan, and push notification preferences for deals and campaigns.

## The Digital Membership Card

Every Landmark Rewards member has a digital membership card available under the "Rewards" tab of any brand's app (Lifestyle, Max, Splash, Home Centre, Centrepoint, or Babyshop), since all six brands share the same underlying Landmark Rewards account. The digital card displays a unique QR code and a fallback numeric barcode, either of which can be scanned at any till across all six brands to identify the member's account for that transaction. The card also displays the member's current tier (Silver, Gold, or Platinum), current points balance, and the points balance due to expire in the next 30 days, matching the expiry notification described in the Loyalty Program FAQ.

Landmark Group does not issue a physical plastic membership card by default; the digital card in the app is the primary and default form factor. Customers without a smartphone, or who prefer a physical card, can request one at any till, and it is issued instantly using the same account number linked to their mobile number, with no additional fee.

## Scan-to-Earn Process In-Store

At checkout, the cashier scans the member's QR code (or manually enters the numeric barcode/mobile number if the scanner cannot read it) before finalizing the transaction. Scanning must happen before payment is completed and the receipt printed, since the points calculation is tied to the finalized transaction total. The member needs to unlock their phone and open the app to the Rewards tab, or use the relevant brand's home-screen widget where available, for the cashier to scan it.

Online orders do not require a manual scan: as long as the customer is logged into their Landmark Rewards-linked account when placing the order, points are calculated and credited automatically to the same account without any separate action needed.

## Forgetting the App or Card at Checkout

If a customer completes an in-store purchase without scanning their membership card or providing their linked mobile number, the transaction is processed as a non-member sale and does not automatically earn points, since the till has no way to associate the sale with an account after the fact. However, Landmark Rewards allows retroactive point crediting within a 7-day window from the date of purchase, provided the customer has the original physical receipt (or a digital receipt reference, where the store offers e-receipts) showing the transaction date, store, and amount. The customer can submit a retroactive crediting request through the "Missing Points" option in the app's Rewards tab, uploading a photo of the receipt, or by visiting the original store with the receipt.

Requests submitted after the 7-day window are not eligible for retroactive crediting under standard policy, since receipt validation and fraud-prevention checks become materially harder to run reliably beyond that window. CX agents may use judgment on a case-by-case exception basis for a long-standing active member with a documented purchase pattern, but this is a discretionary goodwill exception rather than a standing entitlement, typically handled at the Tier 2 escalation level described in the Complaint Escalation Process rather than by a first-line agent.

## Push Notification Preferences

The app sends several categories of push notification by default upon enrollment: points-expiry alerts (30 days before expiry, as described in the Loyalty Program FAQ), tier-change notifications, order and delivery status updates, and marketing notifications for sales, bonus-point campaigns, and new arrivals. Each category can be independently toggled on or off under Account Settings > Notification Preferences; a customer can, for example, keep order-status and points-expiry alerts active while opting out of general marketing pushes.

Transactional notifications (order confirmations, delivery updates, points-expiry alerts) cannot be fully disabled while a customer has an active account and pending or recent orders, since these are essential account communications rather than marketing; only the marketing/deals category can be fully switched off. Customers wanting to stop all communication entirely would need to close their Landmark Rewards account rather than adjust settings.

## Multi-Device Access and Scanner Troubleshooting

A Landmark Rewards account can be logged into and used from multiple devices simultaneously (for example, a phone and tablet both showing the same digital card), since the card is tied to the account rather than a specific device installation. If a member loses access to their phone, the digital card can be retrieved by logging in from a different device using the registered mobile number and OTP verification, or a physical card can be requested in-store as an interim solution.

If a till scanner fails to read a member's QR code (low screen brightness, a cracked screen, or a scratched physical card are common causes), the cashier can manually key in the member's registered mobile number to pull up the account, which is treated as equivalent to a successful scan for points-earning purposes. A scanner malfunction should therefore never be a reason a member misses out on earning points; CX escalation for a "points not credited due to scanner issue" complaint should first confirm whether the cashier attempted the manual lookup before treating it as a missed-points case requiring the 7-day retroactive crediting process.
