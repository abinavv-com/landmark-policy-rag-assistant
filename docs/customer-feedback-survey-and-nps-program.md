# Customer Feedback Survey and NPS Program

This document covers how and when customers are surveyed after a purchase or service interaction, the incentive offered for completing a survey, and how survey results feed into the CX escalation process described in the Complaint Escalation Process.

## Survey Timing and Channels

Landmark runs a standard post-purchase Net Promoter Score (NPS) survey, sent via app push notification and email, triggered 3 days after an order is marked "Delivered" for home-delivery orders, or 1 day after collection for click-and-collect orders. The shorter interval for click-and-collect reflects that the customer has already physically received and can immediately assess the item, whereas a delivered order allows a short settling period for the customer to unpack and use the item before being asked to rate the experience. Customers who contact CX for a service interaction (a return, a complaint, or a warranty claim) receive a separate, shorter service-specific satisfaction survey within 24 hours of the case being marked resolved, distinct from the standard post-purchase NPS survey, since it measures the service interaction itself rather than the underlying product or delivery experience.

Surveys are sent at most once per order and once per resolved service case; a customer with multiple orders or cases in a short period may receive multiple separate survey prompts, but Landmark caps total survey volume at no more than 3 survey prompts per customer per rolling 30-day period to avoid survey fatigue, with lower-priority prompts (typically the standard post-purchase NPS survey, as opposed to a service-case survey) suppressed first when the cap is reached.

## Survey Format

The core NPS question asks the customer to rate, on a 0-10 scale, how likely they are to recommend the specific brand (Lifestyle, Max, Splash, Home Centre, Centrepoint, or Babyshop) to a friend or colleague, followed by an optional open-text field for comments. The service-specific satisfaction survey additionally asks the customer to rate the specific CX interaction (resolution speed, agent helpfulness, and whether the issue was fully resolved) on a 1-5 scale. Both survey types take under 60 seconds to complete and do not require the customer to log in separately, since the survey link is pre-authenticated to the specific order or case it relates to.

## Survey Completion Incentive

Customers who complete either survey type receive a small Landmark Rewards bonus point credit — 20 points for the standard post-purchase NPS survey, or 30 points for the service-specific satisfaction survey, reflecting the additional time typically needed to answer the extra service-rating question. This bonus is credited automatically to the customer's Landmark Rewards account within 24 hours of survey submission and does not require the customer to be logged in with a specific tier; the flat point value applies uniformly regardless of Silver, Gold, or Platinum status. The incentive is not paid for a partially completed survey (for example, abandoning after the numeric rating but before any optional comment); the numeric rating alone is sufficient to trigger the credit, since the open-text comment field is genuinely optional and not required for the bonus.

## How Survey Results Feed the Escalation Process

A response scoring 0-6 on the NPS scale (a "detractor" response) or 1-2 on the service satisfaction scale automatically flags the case for proactive CX follow-up, distinct from and in addition to whatever resolution the customer already received. This follow-up is routed to a specialized recovery queue, separate from the standard Tier 1 first-line queue described in the Complaint Escalation Process, and a recovery agent reaches out within 24 hours to understand the specific issue behind the low score, even if the customer did not file a separate complaint through the standard channels. Where the low-score follow-up uncovers an unresolved underlying issue, the case is escalated directly into Tier 2 of the standard Complaint Escalation Process, using the survey response itself as the documented starting point for that escalation rather than requiring the customer to re-explain the issue from scratch.

A response scoring 9-10 (a "promoter" response) with a positive open-text comment naming a specific store associate or CX agent is forwarded to that individual's team lead as positive recognition, separate from the compensation-focused escalation track, as part of Landmark's internal service-quality recognition process.

## Aggregate Reporting and Program Governance

Individual survey responses are also aggregated at the brand, market, and store level to produce a rolling NPS score used internally for service-quality tracking and is not shared externally or used to make individual customer-facing decisions beyond the detractor follow-up described above. A customer's specific survey response does not affect their Landmark Rewards tier, their standing to make future purchases, or their eligibility for other services; it is used exclusively for service-quality follow-up and internal reporting.
