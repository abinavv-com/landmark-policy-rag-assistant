# International Shipping and Customs FAQ

Landmark Group's primary retail operations run in the UAE, KSA, and India, but a subset of brands also fulfill orders to additional international destinations. This document covers customs responsibility, clearance delays, restricted items, and checkout pricing for cross-border orders.

## Destinations Served Beyond UAE, KSA, and India

Beyond the three core markets, Lifestyle, Max, and Home Centre online storefronts ship to the other GCC markets (Kuwait, Bahrain, Oman, Qatar) as part of the same regional distribution network used for standard Gulf shipping. Beyond the GCC, a limited international shipping program covers the United Kingdom, and select markets in Southeast Asia (Malaysia, Singapore) and East Africa (Kenya) where Landmark Group has an established retail or franchise presence. Splash, Centrepoint, and Babyshop currently ship internationally only within the GCC and do not yet support the UK/Southeast Asia/East Africa lane; customers in those markets can only purchase Splash, Centrepoint, or Babyshop merchandise through a local franchise partner storefront where one exists, not through the UAE or India online store.

Availability of international shipping to a given country is shown at checkout based on the delivery address entered; not every product category is eligible for every destination, since some categories (notably furniture and large appliances) are excluded from long-haul international shipping due to freight cost and breakage risk.

## Customs Duties and Responsibility

For all cross-border orders outside the customer's checkout-country tax jurisdiction, customs duties, import taxes, and any local clearance fees are the responsibility of the recipient, not Landmark Group. These charges are levied by the destination country's customs authority at the point of import and are not included in the product price or shipping fee shown at checkout, since duty rates and thresholds vary by destination and by product category (apparel, electronics, and cosmetics are frequently taxed at different rates within the same country).

Where Landmark's courier partner offers a delivered-duty-paid (DDP) option at checkout, prepaying estimated duties is possible for select UK and GCC-to-GCC lanes; if DDP is not offered for a given destination, the shipment moves as delivered-duty-unpaid (DDU) and the courier will collect duties directly from the recipient before or at delivery. Orders refused at customs due to unpaid duties are returned to the origin warehouse, and the customer is refunded the product value only, not the original outbound shipping fee, since that cost was already incurred regardless of the refusal.

## Estimated Customs Clearance Delays

Customs clearance timelines are in addition to the transit time already reflected in the shipping estimate. GCC-to-GCC shipments typically clear customs within 1 business day given the harmonized Gulf customs framework. Shipments to the UK typically add 2-4 business days for clearance, and shipments to Southeast Asia or East Africa can add 4-7 business days, depending on how frequently the destination customs office processes low-value retail parcels versus commercial freight. Customers should treat the checkout-stage delivery estimate as transit time only and expect clearance delays on top of it for any destination outside the GCC.

## Restricted and Prohibited Items by Destination

Certain product categories cannot be shipped to specific destinations due to import regulation:

- Cosmetics and fragrances containing alcohol are restricted for import into several GCC-adjacent and Southeast Asian jurisdictions and are automatically excluded from the cart when the delivery address falls under a restricted destination.
- Leather and animal-derived materials (including certain footwear and bags) face additional import documentation requirements for East Africa and are held at customs pending manufacturer certification in some cases, which can extend clearance well beyond the standard estimate.
- Battery-containing electronics (including some Home Centre small appliances) are subject to air-freight battery handling restrictions and may be re-routed to sea freight for longer-haul destinations, extending delivery by 2-3 weeks in those cases.
- Children's items containing small parts are subject to additional safety-compliance checks for UK-bound shipments under UK product safety regulation, which can add clearance time beyond the standard UK estimate.

The checkout flow will not allow a restricted item to be added to a cart for an ineligible destination; if a restriction is discovered only after the order is placed (due to a destination change or a customs hold), CX proactively contacts the customer with a refund or reroute-to-eligible-address option.

## Currency and Pricing at Checkout

Cross-border orders display pricing in the currency of the destination country where Landmark Group has an active local pricing catalog (AED for GCC destinations served from the UAE hub, SAR for KSA-originated orders, INR for India-originated orders). For destinations outside these three currencies (UK, Southeast Asia, East Africa), checkout displays an estimated price converted to the local currency at the prevailing exchange rate at time of order, clearly marked as an estimate; the actual charge to the customer's card is processed in AED (or the origin warehouse's base currency) and may vary slightly from the displayed estimate due to the card network's own conversion rate and any foreign transaction fee charged by the customer's bank, which is outside Landmark's control.
