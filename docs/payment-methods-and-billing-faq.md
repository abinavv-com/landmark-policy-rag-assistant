# Payment Methods and Billing FAQ

This document covers accepted payment methods, billing timelines, and refund handling across Landmark Group online and in-store checkouts in the UAE, KSA, and India, spanning Lifestyle, Max, Splash, Home Centre, Centrepoint, and Babyshop.

## Accepted Payment Methods by Region

**UAE and KSA**: Visa, Mastercard, and American Express are accepted both online and in-store. Apple Pay is available at checkout on the mobile app and mobile web for supported devices, and at contactless-enabled store tills. Buy-now-pay-later is available through both Tabby and Tamara, letting customers split a purchase into 4 interest-free installments, subject to each provider's own approval check at checkout, which is instant in the vast majority of cases. Cash on delivery (COD) is available on UAE and KSA online orders up to AED 1,500 / SAR 1,500 per order; orders above this threshold must use a card, wallet, or BNPL method.

**India**: Visa, Mastercard, RuPay, and American Express cards are accepted, along with UPI (all major apps), net banking, and popular mobile wallets. EMI is available on orders above INR 3,000 when paid by a credit card from a participating bank, with tenures of 3, 6, 9, or 12 months; the interest rate is set by the issuing bank, not by Landmark, and is disclosed at checkout before the customer confirms EMI. Cash on delivery is available up to INR 15,000 per order, with a flat COD handling fee of INR 49, waived for Landmark Rewards Gold and Platinum members.

Gift cards and Landmark Rewards points can be combined with any of the above payment methods as a partial payment, but cannot fully substitute for a payment method on COD orders, since some form of card, wallet, or cash confirmation is required to validate a COD booking.

## Billing and Refund Timelines by Method

Refund timing depends on the original payment method, since Landmark has no control over how quickly a customer's bank or wallet provider posts the credit:

- **Cards (Visa/Mastercard/Amex)**: 5-7 business days for the refund to reflect, though the customer's issuing bank may take a further billing cycle to show it on a statement.
- **Apple Pay**: Follows the same 5-7 business day timeline as the underlying card linked to the Apple Pay wallet.
- **UPI / net banking / mobile wallets (India)**: 3-5 business days.
- **Tabby / Tamara BNPL**: The refund is issued to the BNPL provider, which cancels or adjusts the remaining installment schedule; this typically completes within 5-7 business days, and any installments already collected are refunded to the customer's original card by the BNPL provider directly, not by Landmark.
- **Cash on delivery**: Refunded via bank transfer, requiring the customer to submit IBAN (UAE/KSA) or bank account and IFSC details (India); this takes 7-10 business days to settle.
- **Landmark Rewards points or store credit used as full/partial payment**: The points or credit portion is restored to the customer's account instantly upon refund approval, with only the remaining cash portion (if any) following the timelines above.

## Failed Payment and Retry Policy

If a payment attempt fails at checkout (declined card, expired BNPL approval, or a wallet timeout), the order is held in "Payment Pending" status for 30 minutes, during which the customer can retry with the same or a different payment method without losing their cart or any applied promo code. After 30 minutes, the reserved stock is released back into inventory and the customer must re-add items and check out again.

Customers are allowed up to 3 payment retry attempts per checkout session before the session is locked for security reasons; a locked session requires starting a fresh order. If a card is charged but the order does not confirm due to a gateway timeout, the pending charge is automatically reversed by the payment processor within 3-5 business days; Landmark's CX team can expedite verification of this reversal if the customer raises a ticket with the bank reference number.

## Currency Conversion for Cross-Border Purchases

All Landmark storefronts bill in the local currency of the storefront being shopped (AED for UAE, SAR for KSA, INR for India). If a customer's card is issued by a bank outside that country, the card network (Visa/Mastercard/Amex) or issuing bank applies its own currency conversion rate and, in most cases, a foreign transaction fee, at the time of settlement; this conversion is entirely outside Landmark's pricing and is not reflected in the price shown at checkout, which is always the local-currency amount.

Refunds to cross-border cards are issued in the original transaction currency (AED, SAR, or INR) and reconverted by the card issuer at the prevailing rate on the refund date, which may differ slightly from the original purchase-date rate. This exchange rate variance is standard across the payment industry and is not something Landmark's CX team can adjust or compensate for.

## Billing Support and Disputes

If a customer disputes a charge (duplicate charge, incorrect amount, or an unrecognized transaction), CX opens an investigation with the payment processor within 2 business days. Confirmed duplicate or erroneous charges are refunded within the standard method-based timelines above once the investigation closes, typically 5-10 business days from the initial report. Customers should raise a bank chargeback only if the CX-led investigation does not resolve within 15 business days, since a bank-initiated chargeback can temporarily freeze the customer's Landmark Rewards account pending resolution.
