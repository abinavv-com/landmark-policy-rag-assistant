# Furniture Assembly and Installation Service Terms

This document covers Home Centre's paid assembly and installation service: what is included in the base service fee, what counts as a chargeable add-on, scheduling windows, and liability for installation-related damage. It applies only to Home Centre, since assembly and installation are not relevant service lines for the apparel-focused brands.

## What the Base Service Includes

Home Centre offers paid assembly and installation for furniture and select homeware categories purchased online or in-store: flat-pack furniture (wardrobes, bookshelves, bed frames, dining sets), modular seating, and standard wall-shelving units. The base fee, quoted at checkout or point of sale based on item category and complexity, covers unboxing, assembly per manufacturer specification, placement of the assembled item in the room of the customer's choice, and removal of packaging materials from the premises afterward. Assembly is carried out by a Home Centre-vetted third-party installation partner, not the delivery courier, and is booked as a separate line item rather than bundled automatically into every furniture purchase.

## Add-On Services Not Included in the Base Fee

The following are treated as chargeable add-ons beyond the base assembly fee, quoted separately at the time of booking:

1. **Wall-mounting**, including TV wall-mounts, floating shelves, and wall-hung storage units, since this requires drilling and wall-anchor assessment that a standard flat-pack assembly does not.
2. **Disposal of old furniture** being replaced by the new purchase, charged per item removed and subject to local municipal disposal regulations in each market; the installation team will not remove old furniture unless this add-on is pre-booked, since unscheduled removal is not accounted for in the visit's time slot or the partner's vehicle capacity.
3. **Electrical connection work**, such as hardwiring a light fixture or connecting an appliance to a dedicated circuit, which requires a licensed electrician rather than the standard furniture installation partner and is quoted and scheduled as a separate service request.
4. **Assembly of items not purchased from Home Centre**, which is not offered under this service at all; the installation partner network is contracted specifically for Home Centre-sold products and does not take on third-party furniture assembly jobs.

Customers should confirm which add-ons they need at the time of booking, since add-ons requested on the day of the appointment (for example, asking the installer to also mount a TV that was not part of the original booking) may not be accommodated within the scheduled visit and will require a follow-up appointment.

## Scheduling Windows

Assembly and installation appointments are offered in half-day windows — morning (9:00 AM-1:00 PM) or afternoon (1:00 PM-6:00 PM) — selected at checkout or by calling the Home Centre services line for in-store purchases. The installation team does not commit to a narrower slot than the half-day window, given the variability in assembly duration across furniture types, but sends an SMS notification 30-60 minutes before arrival. Standard scheduling lead time is 3-7 business days from the delivery date, depending on regional installer availability; large-format items (full bedroom sets, modular sectionals) may need up to 10 business days outside major metro hubs.

Rescheduling is free of charge if requested at least 24 hours before the appointment window; rescheduling requested with less than 24 hours' notice, or a missed appointment where the customer is not present to grant access, is subject to a re-visit fee, since the installer's time slot and travel for that day cannot be recovered.

## Liability for Installation Damage

Home Centre's installation partner is liable for damage caused directly by the installation process, covering three scenarios:

- **Damage to the furniture item itself** during assembly (for example, a cracked panel from incorrect assembly technique), which is remedied by a free replacement part or full item replacement at Home Centre's discretion, at no cost to the customer.
- **Damage to the customer's property** during assembly or wall-mounting (a wall scuff, a dented floor, or a damaged doorway from maneuvering a large item), assessed by the installation partner's supervisor within 3 business days of the customer reporting it, with approved claims compensated via repair reimbursement or a goodwill store credit, per the general approach in the Complaint Escalation Process.
- **Pre-existing property conditions**, such as weak or hollow wall material unsuitable for a wall-mount, or existing structural issues unrelated to the installation, which are explicitly excluded from liability; the installer will flag a wall or surface they assess as unsuitable before proceeding and may decline to complete a wall-mount installation on that basis rather than proceed and risk a failure.

Damage claims must be reported within 48 hours of the installation visit, since delayed reporting makes it difficult to distinguish installation-caused damage from unrelated, later-occurring damage. Claims reported after this window are handled on a case-by-case basis through the standard Complaint Escalation Process rather than the expedited installation-liability track.

## Cancelling a Booked Assembly Service

The assembly service fee can be cancelled and refunded in full up to 24 hours before the scheduled appointment window. Cancellations inside the 24-hour window are refunded at 50% of the service fee, reflecting the installer slot already allocated and typically unable to be rebooked with another customer on short notice. If the underlying furniture item itself is returned under the standard Returns and Exchange Policy before the assembly appointment takes place, the assembly fee is refunded in full regardless of the cancellation timing, since there is no longer an item to assemble.
