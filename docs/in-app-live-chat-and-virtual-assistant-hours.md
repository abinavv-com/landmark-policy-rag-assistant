# In-App Live Chat and Virtual Assistant Hours

This document covers the availability hours for human live-chat agents, what the automated virtual assistant (chatbot) can and cannot resolve on its own, and the response-time expectations for chat compared to email. It should be read alongside the Complaint Escalation Process, which covers the Tier 1 chat SLA of 2 hours during standard operating hours; this document expands on the hours themselves and the chatbot-to-human handoff mechanics that sit ahead of that SLA.

## Human Live-Chat Agent Hours

Human live-chat agents are available across all three markets during the standard CX operating window of 8:00 AM to 11:00 PM local time, seven days a week, consistent with the operating hours referenced in the Complaint Escalation Process. Within this window, a customer opening the in-app or website chat is connected to a live agent (after any initial chatbot triage, described below) with the standard 2-hour Tier 1 response SLA. Outside this window — overnight, roughly 11:00 PM to 8:00 AM local time — the chat interface remains accessible, but messages are handled by the virtual assistant only; a customer requiring human agent support outside these hours has their case queued and picked up by the first available live agent once the window reopens, typically with a follow-up notification once a human response is ready rather than requiring the customer to keep the chat window open overnight.

There is currently no difference in live-agent operating hours between the UAE, KSA, and India markets; all three run the same 8:00 AM-11:00 PM local-time window, staffed by region-specific agent teams so that local-language support (Arabic, English, and Hindi, depending on market) is available throughout the window rather than only during a subset of hours.

## Virtual Assistant (Chatbot) Capabilities

The virtual assistant is available 24/7 in all three markets and is the first point of contact for any chat session, whether opened during or outside live-agent hours. It is designed to resolve, without human involvement, the categories of request that make up the bulk of routine CX volume:

- Order status and tracking lookups, pulling directly from the order management system.
- Standard policy questions with a clear, non-ambiguous answer — return windows, shipping fees, gift card balance checks, and Landmark Rewards points balance and expiry lookups.
- Initiating a standard return or exchange request for an eligible order, including generating a return label, without needing a human agent to process a straightforward, policy-compliant return.
- Basic account actions such as password reset initiation and updating a saved delivery address.

The chatbot is intentionally not designed to make judgment calls or exceptions: it does not process goodwill compensation, cannot approve a return outside the stated policy window, and cannot resolve a dispute where the customer disagrees with information the system shows (for example, a customer insisting a package was not delivered despite tracking showing "Delivered"). In these cases, the chatbot's role is limited to correctly triaging the request rather than attempting to resolve it.

## Chatbot-to-Human Handoff

The virtual assistant hands a conversation off to a human agent automatically under any of the following conditions: the customer's message indicates dissatisfaction or explicitly asks for a human agent, the request falls into a category the bot is not designed to resolve (an exception request, a dispute, or a complaint), or the bot fails to correctly classify the customer's intent after two consecutive attempts within the same conversation, to avoid trapping a customer in an unproductive back-and-forth. When a handoff occurs during live-agent hours, the conversation transfers to the Tier 1 queue with the bot's conversation transcript already attached, so the customer does not need to repeat information already provided. When a handoff occurs outside live-agent hours, the conversation is queued for the next available agent at window opening, per the overnight handling described above.

## Response-Time Comparison: Chat vs. Email

Live chat carries a materially faster response SLA than email, and CX actively steers customers toward chat for time-sensitive issues:

- **Live chat (human agent, during hours)**: 2-hour response SLA, as set out in the Complaint Escalation Process, though in practice the large majority of chat sessions during hours are picked up by an agent within a few minutes given real-time queue management.
- **Virtual assistant (any time)**: effectively instant for requests within its resolvable scope, since no human queue is involved.
- **Email**: 24-hour response SLA, reflecting that email is treated as an asynchronous channel rather than a real-time one; email is generally recommended for non-urgent requests or when a customer needs to attach multiple documents or images that are easier to compile outside a live chat window.

Customers submitting the same request through both channels simultaneously (for example, emailing and opening a chat about the same issue) do not receive two independent SLA clocks; agents merge the case under whichever channel responds first and close the duplicate to avoid conflicting responses.

## Social Media and Other Channels

Complaints or queries raised via social media follow the priority social care SLA described in the Complaint Escalation Process (1-hour acknowledgment during operating hours) rather than the standard chat or email SLA, and are not handled by the in-app chatbot or live-chat queue at all, since social media messages are routed to a separate, dedicated social care team.
