# Environmental and Social Governance Supplier Standards

This document covers Landmark Group's supplier code of conduct, factory audit and sourcing selection process, and customer-facing ESG transparency reporting, applicable to sourcing across all six brands (Lifestyle, Max, Splash, Home Centre, Centrepoint, Babyshop). This document is distinct from the customer-facing Sustainability and Recycling Program document, which covers in-store take-back, packaging, and e-waste programs; this document instead covers upstream supplier and sourcing standards.

## Supplier Code of Conduct

All manufacturing suppliers and factories producing goods for any Landmark Group brand are required to sign and operate under Landmark's supplier code of conduct as a condition of doing business, covering baseline labor standards including a prohibition on child labor and forced labor, minimum wage compliance with local law, a maximum standard working week with legally compliant overtime compensation, freedom of association, and basic workplace health and safety requirements such as fire safety egress, machine guarding, and access to clean drinking water and sanitation facilities. The code of conduct applies uniformly across Landmark's sourcing base regardless of which country a factory is located in or which brand the goods are destined for, rather than varying by brand or market.

Suppliers are also required to cascade equivalent labor and safety standards to any subcontractor used for a portion of the production process (such as a specialized embroidery or finishing subcontractor), and undisclosed subcontracting discovered during an audit is treated as a code-of-conduct violation in its own right, independent of whether the subcontractor itself is found to be compliant on inspection.

## Factory Audits

New suppliers undergo an initial compliance audit, conducted by Landmark's sourcing compliance team or an accredited third-party social-compliance auditor, before being approved to begin production for any Landmark brand. Approved suppliers are subject to periodic re-audits, with frequency depending on audit history and risk category: lower-risk suppliers with a clean history are typically re-audited every 12-24 months, while higher-risk categories (factories with a prior corrective-action finding, or new subcontractor relationships) are audited more frequently until a sustained clean record is established.

Audit findings are categorized by severity, from minor administrative gaps (incomplete recordkeeping) to major or critical findings (a safety hazard or labor-standard violation). Major and critical findings require a time-bound corrective action plan, and continued sourcing is contingent on demonstrated progress; a critical finding not remediated within the required window can result in supplier suspension until compliance is verified, or termination from Landmark's approved supplier list in serious or repeat cases.

## Selecting Suppliers for Sustainable Product Lines

As referenced in the Sustainability and Recycling Program document, products carrying the "Conscious Choice" green leaf hangtag (organic cotton, recycled polyester, or certified responsible sourcing such as better cotton or FSC-certified wood) are sourced from a subset of suppliers who additionally hold the relevant third-party sustainability certification for that specific material claim, verified through certification documentation reviewed by the sourcing compliance team before a product is approved to carry the label. Meeting the standard supplier code of conduct is a baseline requirement for all suppliers regardless of whether they produce Conscious Choice-labeled goods; the additional sustainability certification is a further, material-specific qualification layered on top of code-of-conduct compliance, not a substitute for it.

Selection of a supplier for an expanded sustainable-line order (for example, scaling up an organic cotton range at Lifestyle or an FSC-certified furniture line at Home Centre) also factors in the supplier's audit history and corrective-action track record, since Landmark's sourcing team treats social compliance and environmental material certification as separate but jointly required criteria — a supplier with strong environmental certification but an open major labor-standard finding would not be approved for expanded sustainable-line sourcing until the labor finding is resolved.

## Customer-Facing Transparency Reporting

Landmark publishes an annual ESG summary report, covering supplier audit program metrics (such as the proportion of the active supplier base audited in the reporting year and the resolution rate of major findings), sourcing certification progress for sustainable product lines, and the packaging and recycling metrics also referenced in the customer-facing Sustainability and Recycling Program document. This annual report is published on Landmark Group's corporate website rather than on individual brand storefronts, and is aimed primarily at institutional stakeholders, investors, and corporate customers rather than being surfaced directly within the shopping app or website experience.

Customers asking a CX agent for detailed factory-level audit data or a specific supplier's compliance status should be told that Landmark does not disclose factory-specific or supplier-specific audit findings publicly, consistent with standard practice across the retail sector for protecting supplier commercial confidentiality and audit-process integrity, but that aggregate program-level metrics are available in the published annual ESG summary for customers who want visibility into the program's overall scope and outcomes.

## Handling Customer Ethical-Sourcing Complaints

If a customer raises a specific ethical-sourcing concern about a product (for example, citing external media coverage of a factory or region), the case should be logged and routed to the sourcing compliance team for review rather than resolved by CX directly, since verifying or responding to a specific sourcing allegation requires access to supplier audit records that sit outside standard CX tooling. CX should acknowledge the concern, confirm it has been logged for sourcing compliance review, and set expectations that a substantive response may take longer than the standard complaint-escalation timelines described in the Complaint Escalation Process, given the internal verification required before Landmark can respond definitively to an external sourcing allegation.
