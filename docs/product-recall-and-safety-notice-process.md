# Product Recall and Safety Notice Process

This document describes how Landmark Group communicates a product recall or safety notice, the refund/replacement process for affected items, and specific handling for Babyshop child-safety recalls, across all six brands (Lifestyle, Max, Splash, Home Centre, Centrepoint, Babyshop) in the UAE, KSA, and India.

## When a Recall Is Issued

A product recall or safety notice is issued when Landmark's sourcing compliance or quality assurance team, a regulatory authority, or a manufacturer identifies that a specific product batch poses a safety risk — examples include a mechanical failure risk in Home Centre furniture hardware, a fire-safety concern in an appliance, a choking hazard in a Babyshop toy, or a labeling/allergen error on a feeding product. Recalls are distinct from routine quality complaints handled through the Damaged or Defective Items Policy: a recall applies to an entire identified batch or production run regardless of whether an individual customer's unit has shown a problem, whereas a standard damaged-item claim is assessed unit by unit.

## How a Recall Is Communicated

Once a recall is confirmed, Landmark notifies affected customers through multiple channels simultaneously to maximize reach:

1. **Direct email and SMS** to any customer whose purchase history shows the affected product, using batch or SKU data captured at the point of sale (online order records always capture this; in-store purchases are matched where the Landmark Rewards account was linked to the transaction).
2. **In-app notification**, pushed as a priority alert that bypasses marketing-notification opt-out settings, since safety notices are treated as an essential communication category similar to order-status updates.
3. **In-store signage** at the point of sale and customer service counters in stores that carried the item, for customers who may not be reachable through a linked account (for example, a cash purchase).
4. **A dedicated recall notice page**, linked from the footer of each affected brand's app and website, where any customer can check whether a specific SKU or batch number is subject to an active recall.

## Refund or Replacement Process for Recalled Items

Recalled items are eligible for a full refund or a like-for-like replacement (once a corrected batch is available) regardless of how long ago the item was purchased, overriding the standard 30-day return window (and the shorter Max, Splash, and Home Centre sale-item windows) described in the Returns and Exchange Policy. There is no requirement for the item to be unused, unwashed, or in original packaging with tags attached, since the safety issue exists independent of the item's condition, and in many cases the customer is specifically advised to stop using the item rather than continue using it until return.

Customers can process a recall return at any store location or through a dedicated reverse-pickup request via CX for online purchases, without needing the original receipt if the item and its batch/SKU number match the recall notice, since the recall itself is the basis for eligibility rather than standard proof-of-purchase. Refunds are issued to the original payment method by default; store credit is not offered as a substitute unless the customer specifically requests it, since a safety-driven return should not carry any risk of feeling pressured toward store credit over cash back.

## Babyshop Child-Safety Recalls

Given the heightened sensitivity of child-safety issues, Babyshop recalls involving hard goods (car seats, cribs, strollers, high chairs) or choking-hazard toy and feeding products follow an accelerated communication timeline: direct customer notification is targeted within 24 hours of a recall being confirmed internally, rather than the standard multi-day rollout used for lower-severity recalls in other categories. Babyshop CX is also briefed with a specific script for these cases that prioritizes clear, non-alarmist safety guidance (what to do with the item right now, such as discontinuing use immediately) ahead of the refund logistics discussion, since the immediate safety guidance matters more in the first customer interaction than the administrative resolution.

As referenced in the Babyshop Brand FAQ, customers who registered a car seat or stroller directly with the manufacturer may also receive a manufacturer-direct recall notice in parallel; both notifications point to the same issue, and a customer only needs to act on one channel to complete the return or replacement.

## Checking If a Purchased Item Is Subject to an Active Recall

Customers who are unsure whether an item they own is affected can check in three ways: (1) the dedicated recall notice page linked from the brand's app/website footer, searchable by product name, SKU, or batch number found on the product label, (2) their Landmark Rewards purchase history in the app, which flags any past purchase matching an active recall with a banner on that order's detail page, or (3) contacting CX directly with the product name and, where available, the batch number from the safety label, which CX can cross-reference against the internal recall database even if the customer no longer has the receipt.

## Recall Cases Combined with a Prior Complaint

If a customer had already raised a complaint about a specific unit before a formal recall was issued for that batch, the case is retroactively reclassified as a recall case rather than a standard defective-item claim, and the customer is offered the fuller recall-based remedy (no receipt or return-window requirement) rather than being limited to whatever the standard damaged-item process would have provided at the time they first complained.
