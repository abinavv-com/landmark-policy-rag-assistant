# Complaint Escalation Process

This document describes the tiered process for escalating a customer complaint that has not been resolved at the first point of contact, across all Landmark Group brands (Lifestyle, Max, Splash, Home Centre, Centrepoint, Babyshop) in the UAE, KSA, and India.

## Tier 1: First-Line Support

The first point of contact for any complaint is the standard CX channel: in-app chat, email support, or the brand helpline. First-line agents are authorized to resolve the large majority of cases directly, including standard returns and refund exceptions, delivery delay investigations, and price-match or loyalty point disputes, without needing to escalate.

Tier 1 response-time SLA is 2 hours for chat during operating hours (8:00 AM-11:00 PM local time across all three markets), and 24 hours for email submissions. If a Tier 1 agent cannot resolve the complaint within a single interaction — for example, because it requires warehouse or courier investigation — the agent opens a case ticket and commits to a follow-up within 3 business days, matching the investigation timelines described in the Shipping and Delivery FAQ and Damaged or Defective Items Policy.

## Tier 2: Floor Manager or Team Lead Escalation

If a complaint is unresolved after the first Tier 1 interaction, or if the customer explicitly requests escalation, the case moves to Tier 2. For in-store complaints, this means requesting the floor manager or duty manager on shift, who has authority to approve exceptions beyond standard policy limits (for example, approving a return outside the stated window in a genuine goodwill case, or authorizing an on-the-spot store credit). For online complaints, Tier 2 is a senior CX agent or team lead with equivalent override authority.

Tier 2 response-time SLA is same-day for in-store escalations (since the floor manager is physically present) and 24 hours for online Tier 2 escalations. Tier 2 agents are expected to either resolve the case or provide the customer with a clear timeline and next step, rather than returning the case to a general queue.

## Tier 3: Formal Complaints Team

Cases that remain unresolved after Tier 2, or that involve a policy dispute, a safety concern, or a repeated failure on the same order, are escalated to the formal Complaints Team, a centralized group that handles complaints across all six brands and all three markets. Customers can request Tier 3 escalation directly by asking a Tier 1 or Tier 2 agent to "escalate to the complaints team," or by submitting the dedicated complaints form linked in the footer of each brand's website and app.

Tier 3 SLA is an acknowledgment within 24 hours of the case being formally logged, and a substantive resolution or resolution plan within 5 business days. Complex cases involving multiple departments (for example, a warranty dispute that also touches on a delivery failure) may take up to 10 business days, but the Complaints Team is required to provide a status update to the customer at least once every 3 business days during that window rather than going silent.

## Compensation Options

Depending on the nature and severity of the complaint, the resolving tier (Tier 2 or Tier 3, typically not Tier 1) may offer one or more of the following goodwill or corrective measures, in addition to whatever the underlying policy already entitles the customer to (refund, replacement, or repair):

- A goodwill e-voucher, typically in the AED 50-200 range depending on the severity and inconvenience caused, redeemable across all six brands.
- Expedited resolution priority, such as jumping the queue for a replacement shipment or warranty repair.
- Landmark Rewards bonus points credited directly to the member's account as a goodwill gesture, separate from points earned through the original purchase.
- A complimentary alteration, delivery, or setup service waiver for the specific order in question.

Goodwill vouchers are issued at the discretion of Tier 2/3 staff based on documented case severity and are not an automatic entitlement for every escalated complaint; agents are trained to reserve them for cases involving genuine service failure, repeated inconvenience, or a breach of a stated SLA.

## Role of Social Media Complaints

Complaints raised publicly on social media (brand-tagged posts, comments, or direct messages to official Landmark brand accounts) are routed to a dedicated social care queue that operates under a priority SLA: acknowledgment within 1 hour during operating hours, given the visibility and reputational sensitivity of public complaints. Social care agents are empowered to move a case directly to Tier 2 or Tier 3 review, bypassing the standard Tier 1 queue, if the complaint indicates an unresolved case that has already been through first-line support without success.

Raising a complaint on social media does not itself grant a customer additional entitlements beyond policy; it primarily affects the speed and priority of handling, not the underlying resolution the customer is entitled to under the relevant policy (returns, warranty, damaged-item, or shipping policy, as applicable).
