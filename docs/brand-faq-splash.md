# Splash Brand FAQ

Splash is Landmark Group's youth and contemporary fashion brand, built around fast trend cycles and frequent new drops across the UAE, KSA, and India. This supplementary FAQ, maintained by the Splash CX team, covers questions specific to Splash's trend-led retail model.

## Trend-Cycle Speed and New Drops

Splash refreshes its floor assortment considerably faster than Lifestyle, with new trend-led "drops" arriving roughly every 2-3 weeks across core categories such as denim, streetwear-influenced basics, and occasion wear for younger shoppers. This pace means a specific style or print seen on the Splash app or in-store today may not be restocked once sold out, since Splash prioritizes introducing new trend styles over replenishing depleted styles indefinitely. Customers chasing a specific trending item are advised to purchase promptly rather than wait, and to use the "notify me" stock alert on the product page where available.

## Collaboration and Limited-Edition Items

Splash periodically releases limited-edition capsule collections and designer or influencer collaborations, clearly marked with a "Limited Collab" tag on the product page and at the till. Items from these limited collaborations are final sale and are not eligible for return or exchange once purchased, regardless of condition, which is an explicit exception to Splash's standard 21-day sale-item and 30-day full-price return windows described in the Returns and Exchange Policy. This exception exists because limited collaboration runs are produced in very small quantities with no restock, so a returned unit cannot be resold as new-in-season inventory the way a standard markdown item can. The final-sale status is disclosed on the product page and printed on the receipt at the time of purchase, and floor staff are required to confirm the customer has acknowledged it before completing a collab purchase.

## Sale Item Returns

Outside of limited collaborations, Splash follows the group-wide 21-day return window on sale and clearance items, positioned between Max's 14-day window and Lifestyle and Centrepoint's full 30 days. This reflects Splash's trend-fashion model, where clearance turnover is faster than Lifestyle's but not as continuous as Max's near-constant markdown cadence.

## Size Run Availability vs. Lifestyle

As set out in the group-wide Size and Fit Guide, Splash's size chart is closely aligned with Max's rather than Lifestyle's, since both source from overlapping value-fashion supplier bases, and Splash's cuts run smaller than Lifestyle's in the same labeled size, with particularly narrow shoulder cuts on the formal shirting range. Because Splash's trend-led styles are produced in shorter runs than Lifestyle's core basics, popular sizes in a given drop can sell out well before the item is marked down, so customers who are between sizes are encouraged to size up at the time of the initial drop rather than waiting for a restock that may not come. Splash's denim range fits true to its own chart but still runs smaller than Lifestyle's denim, a distinction worth flagging to customers moving between the two brands for the first time.

## Loyalty and Cross-Brand Redemption

Points earned on Splash purchases, including full-price and markdown items, pool into the same unified Landmark Rewards balance shared across all six brands and can be redeemed at Lifestyle, Max, Home Centre, Centrepoint, or Babyshop with no conversion penalty. Limited collaboration items still earn Landmark Rewards points at the standard rate on the price actually paid, even though they are excluded from returns.
