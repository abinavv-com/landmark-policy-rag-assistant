# Marketplace and Third-Party Seller Policy

Alongside first-party inventory sold directly by Lifestyle, Max, Splash, Home Centre, Centrepoint, and Babyshop, Landmark's online storefronts in the UAE, KSA, and India host a curated marketplace of approved third-party sellers, offering complementary categories and long-tail product variety that Landmark does not stock directly. This document explains how marketplace listings differ from first-party inventory, how returns and warranty are handled for marketplace items, and how a dispute is resolved when a marketplace seller does not respond.

## What the Marketplace Is

The Landmark Marketplace program allows vetted third-party sellers — typically specialist brands, regional distributors, or category experts in areas like electronics accessories, niche home decor, or specialty baby products — to list and fulfill their own inventory through Landmark's online storefronts, using Landmark's checkout, payment processing, and customer account infrastructure. Unlike Landmark's own brands, marketplace sellers own, store, and in most cases ship their own stock; Landmark's role is to provide the platform and set baseline standards every seller must meet to remain listed, rather than to hold or fulfill the inventory itself.

Marketplace sellers are onboarded through an approval process that includes business registration verification, a sample product quality review, and agreement to Landmark's marketplace seller terms, which set minimum standards for shipping timelines, customer communication responsiveness, and dispute-handling conduct.

## How to Identify a Marketplace Listing at Checkout

Every marketplace listing is clearly labeled at three points in the shopping journey: on the product listing page, with a "Sold and shipped by [Seller Name]" tag beneath the product title; in the shopping cart, where marketplace items are grouped separately under a "Marketplace Orders" subheading, since they may ship separately and on a different timeline; and on the order confirmation and tracking page, which shows the seller name against each line item. Landmark Rewards points still accrue on marketplace purchases at the standard rate, and gift cards and points can be used as payment exactly as for first-party items, since payment processing runs through Landmark's checkout regardless of who fulfills the order.

## Returns and Warranty: How Marketplace Differs

This is the most important distinction in this document: **the standard Returns and Exchange Policy, Warranty Policy, and Damaged or Defective Items Policy described elsewhere in this knowledge base apply only to first-party Landmark brand inventory.** Marketplace items are governed by the individual seller's own stated return window, condition requirements, and warranty terms, which are displayed on the product listing page and can differ meaningfully from Landmark's standard 30-day window — some marketplace sellers offer a shorter 7 or 14-day window, others match or exceed Landmark's standard terms, and this varies seller by seller.

To initiate a marketplace return, the customer uses the same "My Orders" return flow used for first-party items, but the request routes directly to the seller rather than Landmark's own returns team. The seller approves the return, arranges reverse pickup or return shipping per their own terms, and processes the refund. CX agents fielding a marketplace return query should direct the customer to the seller-specific terms on the original listing rather than quoting the standard Landmark return window, since applying the wrong policy is a common source of frustration on marketplace orders.

## Landmark's Buyer Protection Guarantee

Because Landmark does not directly control marketplace seller fulfillment, it operates a **Buyer Protection Guarantee** as a backstop: if a marketplace order does not arrive within the seller's stated delivery window plus a 5-business-day grace period, or arrives significantly not as described, and the seller does not resolve the issue, Landmark steps in to refund the customer directly, then separately recovers that amount from the seller's marketplace settlement balance. This exists because Landmark's platform, not the seller, is the customer's point of trust at checkout.

## Dispute Resolution When a Seller Does Not Respond

If a marketplace seller does not respond to a return, refund, or quality dispute within **5 business days** of the customer's initial request through the My Orders portal, the case automatically escalates to Landmark's Marketplace Trust and Safety team rather than remaining with the unresponsive seller indefinitely. The team attempts direct contact with the seller for a further 3 business days; if the seller still does not respond, or refuses a resolution the customer is clearly entitled to under the seller's own terms, Landmark invokes the Buyer Protection Guarantee and refunds the customer directly.

Sellers with a pattern of unresolved escalations — measured as more than 3 Trust-and-Safety-escalated cases in a rolling 90-day period — are placed under a formal performance review, which can result in temporary suspension of new listings or, for repeated or severe cases, permanent removal from the Landmark Marketplace program. This performance standard is disclosed to sellers at onboarding as part of the marketplace seller terms they agree to before their first listing goes live.

## Complaint Escalation for Marketplace Orders

Marketplace-specific complaints that a customer wishes to escalate beyond the seller-response window follow the same Tier 1 to Tier 3 structure described in the Complaint Escalation Process, with the Marketplace Trust and Safety team acting as the specialized Tier 2/3 equivalent for these cases rather than a brand's own floor manager or senior CX agent, since marketplace disputes require seller-account-level visibility that standard CX tooling does not provide.
