# Wedding and Occasion Registry Services

This document covers the mechanics of registry completion incentives, guest gifting and duplicate-gift protection, and the transition from a couple's wedding registry to a Babyshop baby registry. For the basics of how a registry is created and which brands participate (Lifestyle and Home Centre for wedding registries, Babyshop for baby registries), see the Festive and Gifting Services FAQ; this document assumes that foundation and focuses on the operational detail CX most frequently fields questions on.

## Setting Up a Bridal Registry

A couple sets up a joint wedding registry through the Lifestyle or Home Centre app, selecting "Create a Registry" under the Services tab, and can add items from both brands' catalogs to a single shared registry link — formalwear accessories, occasion jewelry, and gifting-appropriate homeware from Lifestyle, alongside furniture, dinnerware, and home decor from Home Centre. Both partners can be added as joint account holders on the same registry, each able to edit the item list, so neither partner needs to manage the registry alone. Registries can be set to public (discoverable via a shareable link posted anywhere) or private (accessible only to guests the couple explicitly invites by email or phone number), with private being the default setting to avoid unsolicited registry visits.

## Guest Gifting and Duplicate-Gift Protection

When a guest purchases an item from the registry, the system marks that item (or the relevant quantity, for items registered in multiples such as dinnerware place settings) as fulfilled in real time, so a second guest browsing the same registry link sees an updated availability count rather than purchasing a duplicate. This real-time sync applies whether the guest checks out online or a store associate processes the purchase in-store against the registry using the couple's registry code. In the rare case where two guests purchase the same item within a short window before the sync completes (for example, both checking out within the same few minutes), the couple is notified of the duplicate and can request a store-credit conversion for the second unit rather than returning it, keeping the gift-giver's intent while avoiding an awkward direct return.

Guests do not need a Landmark Rewards account to purchase a registry item, though points from a registry purchase always accrue to the purchasing guest's own account if they are a member, not to the couple's account — consistent with the general registry contribution rule in the Festive and Gifting Services FAQ. Couples can optionally enable an auto-generated thank-you note trigger, which reminds them of the guest's name and gifted item once a purchase is confirmed, to help with post-wedding thank-you card tracking.

## Registry Completion Discount

To encourage couples to use the registry through to their wedding date rather than abandoning it partway, Landmark offers a one-time completion discount: once at least 60% of the registry's total listed value has been purchased by guests, the couple becomes eligible for a 15% discount voucher, valid for 90 days, that they can apply toward purchasing any remaining unpurchased registry items themselves, across both Lifestyle and Home Centre. This voucher is issued automatically to the registry owner's account once the 60% threshold is crossed and is not stackable with the student/staff discount or the Landmark+ 5% discount, following the general single-percentage-discount rule described in the Promotional Code and Coupon Stacking Rules document, though it does combine with Landmark Rewards points redemption. The completion voucher expires unused if the couple does not redeem it within the 90-day window and is not reissued.

## Transitioning to a Babyshop Registry

Couples who created a Lifestyle/Home Centre wedding registry are not automatically enrolled in a Babyshop baby registry when they later expect a child; a new registry must be created separately through the Babyshop app. A couple who links their Babyshop registry to the same account used for their original wedding registry can request the two display together under a single "My Registries" view for convenience, purely as a navigation aid with no functional link between the two gift lists. As noted in the Festive and Gifting Services FAQ, a Babyshop baby registry can already pull in items from Lifestyle's baby apparel range and Home Centre's nursery furniture range within the same link.

## Bridal Event Extras

Lifestyle flagship stores in Dubai, Abu Dhabi, Riyadh, Jeddah, and major Indian metros offer a complimentary in-store bridal trousseau shopping appointment for registry holders, bookable through the same "Book a Styling Session" flow in the Personal Styling and In-Store Services FAQ, flagged in the booking form as being for a registered wedding. This appointment differs from a standard styling session in that it runs a longer 90-minute slot with a consultant familiar with formalwear and occasion-wear ranges, but it carries no different pricing or tier-eligibility rules than the standard complimentary session available to all customers.

## Registry Expiry and Extensions

Consistent with the 12-month active window described in the Festive and Gifting Services FAQ, a wedding registry that has not been extended by the couple is archived 12 months after creation, and the completion discount voucher (if unused) lapses at the same time as the registry archival if the 90-day voucher window has already closed by then. Couples anticipating a postponed wedding date should request an extension through CX before the 12-month mark to avoid losing partially accumulated registry progress toward the 60% completion threshold.
