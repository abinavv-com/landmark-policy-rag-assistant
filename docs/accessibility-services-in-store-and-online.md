# Accessibility Services In-Store and Online

This document covers accessibility commitments and services available to customers with disabilities across Landmark Group brands (Lifestyle, Max, Splash, Home Centre, Centrepoint, Babyshop), both in physical stores and on digital channels, in the UAE, KSA, and India.

## In-Store Physical Accessibility

All Landmark Group flagship and large-format stores (including all Home Centre and Lifestyle flagship locations) provide wheelchair-accessible entrances, ramps, and accessible fitting rooms with wider door clearance. Smaller mall-format and express stores follow the accessibility standards of the host mall, which in most UAE and KSA malls already meet or exceed regional accessibility building codes; store teams can confirm accessible entrance locations for a specific mall on request, since entrance and ramp placement varies by property.

Customers requiring staff assistance while shopping — for guided navigation, product retrieval from high shelves, or reading of price tags and labels — can request help from any available staff member or the customer service desk, and staff are trained to accompany the customer through the shopping journey rather than pointing them toward a general area. This assistance is available without advance booking, though customers planning a longer shopping visit (such as a full wardrobe consultation at Lifestyle or a furniture selection session at Home Centre) can call ahead to the store to have a staff member specifically available at arrival.

## Priority Checkout

Customers with disabilities, along with pregnant customers and customers accompanied by young infants, are entitled to priority checkout at any till, meaning they do not need to wait in the standard queue. This is communicated via visible signage at checkout counters and is honored on request even where signage is not present at a given till. Store staff are instructed not to require documentation or proof of disability to grant priority checkout, since visible need or a customer's own request is sufficient.

## Screen-Reader and Digital Accessibility Commitments

Landmark Group's brand websites and apps are built to align with WCAG 2.1 Level AA guidelines, covering screen-reader compatible navigation, descriptive alt text on product images, keyboard-only navigation support for checkout and account flows, and sufficient color contrast on text and interactive elements. This is an ongoing commitment applied progressively across brand platforms as they are redesigned; customers encountering a specific accessibility barrier on a brand's website or app (a missing alt tag, an unreachable button via keyboard, or a screen-reader compatibility issue) are encouraged to report it through the "Accessibility Feedback" link in the site footer, which routes directly to the digital accessibility team rather than the general CX queue, so it can be triaged and fixed by the product team.

Reported accessibility defects are acknowledged within 3 business days and, where a fix requires development work rather than a content correction, the customer is given an estimated resolution timeframe rather than an open-ended promise, since some fixes depend on a broader release cycle.

## Alternative Formats for Policy Documents

Any published policy document (including the Returns and Exchange Policy, Warranty Policy, and Shipping and Delivery FAQ) is available in alternative formats on request — large-print PDF, plain-text file compatible with screen readers, or a verbal walkthrough over a CX call — at no charge. Requests can be made through the standard CX chat, email, or helpline channel, and are fulfilled within 2 business days for large-print or plain-text formats, or immediately for a verbal walkthrough during a live call.

## Sign-Language-Interpreted Customer Service

Customers who require sign-language support for a customer service interaction can request a scheduled video call with a sign-language interpreter present, coordinated through the CX helpline or the "Accessibility Support" option in the brand app's help section. Because interpreter availability is scheduled rather than on-demand, these sessions are typically booked 1-2 business days in advance; customers with an urgent, time-sensitive issue (such as an active delivery problem) are encouraged to flag urgency at the time of booking so the case can be prioritized for the next available interpreter slot rather than the standard queue.

Sign-language-interpreted sessions can cover any service interaction available through standard CX channels, including returns processing, warranty claims, and formal complaint escalation as described in the Complaint Escalation Process, ensuring customers using this service are not limited to a narrower set of support options than other customers.
