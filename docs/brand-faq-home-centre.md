# Home Centre Brand FAQ

Home Centre is Landmark Group's home and furniture brand, spanning furniture, soft furnishings, decor, and small and major appliances across the UAE, KSA, and India. This supplementary FAQ, maintained by the Home Centre CX team, addresses questions specific to large-item delivery, assembly, and furniture warranty that don't arise for the group's fashion brands.

## Large-Item Delivery Logistics

Furniture and large decor items are delivered via Home Centre's dedicated white-glove delivery service rather than the standard last-mile courier network used for fashion and small-item orders. White-glove delivery includes carrying the item into the customer's home (up to the room of choice, subject to doorway and staircase access), unpacking, and removal of packaging waste, at no additional charge on furniture orders above the applicable free-delivery threshold for that market. Delivery slots for furniture are scheduled in advance with the customer via a call or SMS confirmation, typically within 3-10 business days of order confirmation depending on item availability and whether the piece ships from a regional distribution center or is sourced to order. Customers should ensure doorway and lift access can accommodate the item's packaged dimensions, listed on the product page, since a failed delivery due to access issues may incur a redelivery scheduling fee.

## Assembly Service

Home Centre offers a paid assembly service for furniture requiring on-site construction, such as wardrobes, bed frames, and modular storage units. Assembly is quoted separately from delivery and priced per item based on complexity, shown as an optional add-on at checkout or arranged with the delivery team at the time of scheduling. Customers who decline assembly receive the item flat-packed with manufacturer instructions and standard hardware included. Self-assembled furniture remains covered under Home Centre's standard structural warranty provided assembly followed the manufacturer instructions; damage arising from incorrect self-assembly is treated as a warranty exclusion rather than a covered defect.

## Sale Item Returns

Home Centre applies a 21-day return window on sale and clearance items, as set out in the group-wide Returns and Exchange Policy, positioned between Max's 14-day window and Lifestyle and Centrepoint's full 30 days. Full-priced Home Centre purchases carry the standard 30-day window. Given the size and delivery logistics involved in furniture returns, customers should initiate a return request as early as possible within the window so a reverse pickup can be scheduled, since large-item returns require the same white-glove logistics used for delivery.

## Furniture Warranty

As detailed in the group-wide Warranty Policy, Home Centre furniture (sofas, dining sets, storage units) carries a 24-month structural warranty against manufacturing defects in frames, mechanisms such as recliner or sofa-bed mechanisms, and upholstery seams, explicitly excluding fabric fading from sunlight exposure and normal wear marks on high-traffic upholstery. Small appliances carry a 12-month manufacturer warranty, while major appliances such as refrigerators and washing machines carry the original manufacturer's warranty, typically 24 months, administered directly by the manufacturer's regional service center rather than Home Centre's own CX team. Warranty claims on furniture requiring an at-home inspection are scheduled through the same delivery-logistics team that handles white-glove delivery, given the impracticality of bringing large furniture into a store for inspection.

## Showroom-Only vs. Online-Exclusive Ranges

Certain premium furniture collections and made-to-order upholstery ranges are showroom-only and are not listed for online purchase, since they require in-person fabric and finish selection at a showroom consultation. Conversely, a number of decor, soft furnishings, and small-format storage ranges are online-exclusive and are not stocked on the showroom floor, to keep showroom space focused on larger furniture pieces customers typically want to see in person before buying. The product page for a given item indicates whether it belongs to a showroom-only or online-exclusive range, and showroom-only items can be viewed via a showroom appointment booked through the Home Centre app.
